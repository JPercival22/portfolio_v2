Project Introduction

Background

Come Together Hub Ltd is a heart-centered charity based in St Helens, Merseyside, dedicated to supporting vulnerable individuals in the community. Their mission? To create a safe and empowering space where people can improve their lives through community activities, outreach, and holistic support services.

Before I joined the team, they had worked with a web developer to create a website aimed at boosting their online presence and attracting investments to sustain their vital services. However, the site didnt quite hit the markit lacked polish, wasnt mobile-friendly, and suffered from slow load times, all of which limited its effectiveness in representing the incredible work they do.

Problem

After carefully reviewing the website, it became clear that despite the best intentions, the site had some serious issues. It had been built on an outdated and unmaintained version of WordPress, which left it incomplete and far from functional.

Further analysis uncovered several WordPress-related problems such as file bottlenecks, outdated jQuery and PHP libraries, and JavaScript vulnerabilities that compromised security. In some areas, the front-end simply didnt render at all. More concerning were the discovery of broken and missing code, such as incomplete functions, broken links, and misconfigured scripts, which all contributed to poor performance. To make matters worse, conflicting CSS and media queries caused the site to be unresponsive, delivering a frustrating and inconsistent user experience across all devices.

My Rolls

  • Lead UX Researcher
  • Lead UX & UI Designer
  • Lead Test Facilitator
  • Lead Design System Creator

Solution

After thoroughly assessing the problems with the original website, it became clear that trying to fix it would be more trouble, and expensive, than it was worth. Given that Come Together Hub was still in its early stages and operating with limited financial resources, it was obvious that a new approach was needed.

Moved by the passion and dedication of the organization, I decided to step in and offer to redesign and redevelop the entire website pro-bono. I believed that a charity as committed as Come Together Hub, deserved a website that truly reflected its values and mission, and I was determined to make that happen!

Research

Introduction

When developing the charitys website, our main objective was to create a platform that would attract potential investors and engage the public. We wanted to effectively showcase the organizations mission, upcoming events, and fundraising opportunities. To do this, we conducted in-depth market and user research to get a clear understanding of similar charity websites, industry trends, and what users expect from them. By diving into the motivations, challenges, and preferences of key stakeholders, we crafted a user experience with intuitive navigation and a seamless user journey, designed to meet both the needs of the organization and its audience.

Initial Assumptions

Drawing from my experience, we initially assumed that users would appreciate a clean, streamlined navigation system and a layout based on F and Z-pattern design principles, making content easy to find and digest. We also noted the charitys unique offerings, such as free therapy sessions and a dedicated podcast channel, which led to the decision to integrate video streaming into the site. This feature would boost user engagement, make the experience more personal, and help expand the charitys online presence through subscriptions, as well as links to social media and YouTube.

Research 1.1: Market Research & Competitive Analysis

Understanding the Industry Landscape

Before starting the design and development of Come Together Hubs website, it was essential to gain a deep understanding of the wider online charity landscape. Our aim was to identify industry trends, standard practices, and areas where we could innovate. Through detailed market research and a competitive analysis, we gathered insights that helped us build a website that met industry standards while also addressing the charity's unique needs.

  • Initial Research: We conducted a comprehensive analysis of successful online charity organisations to understand the key elements that engage users and encourage donations.
  • Comparative Analysis: By comparing features across multiple platforms, we pinpointed challenges and opportunities. Most charity websites prominently feature a Hero section and a Donate button on their homepage, ensuring a simple and streamlined donation process.
  • Hero Image Sections: Most charity sites use hero image sections on each page, highlighting their mission, donation calls-to-action, sponsorships, and support links. These sections are placed above the fold for better visibility.
  • Fundraising Methods: We noticed clear differences between the fundraising methods used by smaller and larger organisations:
    • Smaller charities often rely on platforms like GoFundMe due to limited resources.
    • Larger charities tend to use traditional bank transfers and secure online payment methods.
  • Client-Specific Approach: For Come Together Hub, we initially integrated their existing GoFundMe page for donations, with plans to explore additional donation methods as the charity grows.
  • Footer Content: Its a common practice to include essential content like contact information, FAQs, social media links, and sponsor affiliations in the websites footer, so we made sure to follow this standard.
  • Unique Features: Given Come Together Hubs broad range of community services and unique podcast, we sought to combine established charity website standards with a custom, user-friendly design tailored to their specific offerings.

By combining best practices from the charity sector with a tailored approach, we were able to create a website that not only aligns with industry norms but also enhances Come Together Hubs online presence.

Research 1.2: User Interviews & Surveys

Understanding User Expectations

To ensure the platform would meet User expectaions, their needs and provide a seamless experience, we conducted thorough user research. This involved speaking directly with potential users through interviews and surveys, allowing us to gather insights into their behaviours, preferences, and frustrations when navigating current charity websites. This approach allowed us to collect qualitative and quantitative data, which we then triangulated to form our design strategy. We aimed to create a user-centric platform that would resonate with the diverse range of people who engage with Come Together Hub's services.

  • User Research: We conducted a series of in-depth interviews and online surveys, aiming to understand user expectations and needs for charity websites, focusing on key features, ease of use, and overall experience.
  • Diverse Participants: We engaged a varied group of users from different backgrounds, age groups, and levels of digital literacy to capture a wide range of perspectives.
  • Objective: Our aim was to uncover important insights into user behaviours, pain points, and preferences. These findings gave us a better understanding of how people interact with charity platforms and what they find most valuable.
  • Insights Gained: Through this research, we identified common user behaviours and emotions, as well as frustrations with existing charity websites. This validated many of our initial assumptions and provided direct input on what users found confusing or engaging.
  • Affinity Mapping: To organise and synthesise the data, we created an Affinity Mapa method used to group related insights into themes or categories. By clustering similar responses together, we were able to spot patterns and prioritise key areas of improvement and innovation for the site. This mapping process guided our decisions for a user-focused design.
  • Outcome: These insights, along with the affinity mapping, gave us a comprehensive understanding of the challenges users face when navigating charity websites. The findings directly informed our design choices, ensuring that the new platform would provide a seamless, intuitive, and satisfying user experience.

Insights from User Research: Key Findings and Analysis

Visuals

Participants expressed strong enthusiasm for incorporating a video streaming service into the website. They believe this feature will significantly boost user engagement and overall usability, adding a dynamic visual element that captures attention.

Key Information

Users emphasised the need for:

  • Clear & Concise Language: Information should be straightforward and easy to understand.
  • Prominent & Relevant Content: Rich content and media should be strategically placed in sections where they are most impactful, without overwhelming users.
  • Avoiding Information Overload: Its crucial to present information in a digestible manner, steering clear of cluttered or excessive content.

Additionally, users voiced frustration with websites that use guilt-inducing tactics, such as immediate donation prompts or aggressive subscription requests. They favour a more user-friendly approach that accommodates diverse audiences and ensures a smooth, uncomplicated experience.

Overview

These insights have laid a solid foundation for refining the websites design. By integrating this feedback, the design team was able to make informed decisions that enhance user experience, leading to a more engaging and intuitive web service. This approach ensures that the platform not only meets user needs but also provides a more satisfying and effective interaction for Come Together Hubs audience.

Interaction Design 1.2: Front-End Technology Analysis

Given the client's budget constraints for this pro-bono project, engaging a full development team was not financially viable. Instead, I leveraged my expertise in semantic HTML5, CSS3, and modular JavaScript for the development.

Index Page Design

The Index Page introduces globally reusable responsive components to maintain visual consistency across the website. Examples of these are the Navigation, Hero Section, Service Cards, Social Media Sections and the Footer. Albeit, Hero Images change respective and relative to the pages purpose.
This approach ensures a uniform user experience, with familiar interaction patterns that reduce cognitive load and simplify navigation. Reusing components also speeds up development and enables uniform updates, enhancing overall usability and user satisfaction.

Above-The-Fold Objectives

  • Non-intrusive Advertisement (Business Requirement): The main ad is placed below the header, avoiding disruptive pop-ups in line with user preferences.
  • Primary CTA Placement (User Requirement): A primary button above the fold links to the donations page for quick access. Below-the-fold buttons redirect to related internal pages or forms.
  • Secondary CTA Placement (User Requirement): Secondary buttons lead to the Volunteering page and are positioned near the "Donate" button for seamless flow.

Global Component Benefits

  • Consistency: Reusable components create a cohesive experience, reducing user friction.
  • Familiarity: Users easily recognize patterns, making navigation intuitive.
  • Efficiency: Faster updates benefit the entire site with minimal effort.
  • Responsiveness: Components adapt to mobile, tablet, and desktop for optimized viewing.

About Us Page Design

The About Us page balances user expectations with business objectives by offering a transparent presentation in three key sections: a Founders Message, an Information Card Section, and Social Media Links with QR Codes. At the founder's request, the page includes a personal passage highlighting how his own experiences with homelessness inspired the formation of the charity.

Founders Personal Passage Objectives

  • Business/Marketing Requirement: The founders story builds a personal connection with users, enhancing trust and relatability. This approach helps differentiate the charity and engages potential supporters through an authentic and emotional narrative.
  • User Requirement: Detailing how personal experiences bolstred and shapes the charitys mission offers more transparency and a clear understanding of the charitys values and origins, aligning with user expectations for meaningful engagement.

Information and Social Media Link Sections:

  • Information Cards (Business Requirement): Clearly outlining the charitys mission and values builds identity and trust, supporting the goal of establishing a strong, recognizable brand.
  • Concise and Clear Information (Marketing/SEO): Emphasising achievements and community impact enhances credibility and supports marketing efforts to attract donors and increase visibility.
  • Primary Social Media Links (Marketing/SEO): Simplifies access to the charitys social media platforms through interactive, scannable QR codes, promoting updates and events effectively.

Services Page Design

The Services page is designed to offer users easy access to the charity's free services and events. The layout incorporates essential features such as a downloadable timetable, detailed service information, and contextual editorial content. Each service is supported by high-resolution images and venue links, making it simple for users to locate services and plan their participation. By balancing marketing goals and user requirements, this page ensures a smooth and informative experience for the community.

  • Downloadable Services Timetable (Marketing/User Requirement): Offers a regularly updated schedule of events and services provided by the charity, available for free download by the public.
  • Page Editorial (User & Business Requirements): Offers additional context about the pages purpose, providing clarity in case the Hero Section (title and image) isnt sufficient.
  • Information Cards (Marketing/User Requirement): Displays key service details with high-resolution images. Since services are offered at multiple venues, links to each venue and its Google Maps location are included for user convenience.

Volunteer (Contact Us) Page

The Volunteer page is designed with empathy and user-centric principles to encourage engagement. It integrates a compassionate design approach that leverages both visual and textual elements to evoke emotional connections, while the structured form adheres to user experience best practices. The goal is to simplify the sign-up process and reduce barriers, ensuring the form is easy to use and effective in capturing leads.

  • Compassionate Design (Marketing & SEO Strategy): Designed to evoke empathy through supportive imagery and sensitive language, encouraging volunteer sign-ups and donations.
  • Structured Form Standards (User Requirement):
    • Forms only request essential information, displayed logically and clearly to prevent confusion.
    • Input fields are shown in a single column, regardless of screen size, to maintain simplicity and readability.
    • Minimise text inputs, using checkboxes, radio buttons, or dropdowns where possible to reduce user errors.
    • Form labels are positioned above fields, and progressive disclosure techniques are used to enhance interaction.
    • Real-time error detection provides clear, actionable feedback to guide users through form completion.
    • Error messages appear immediately, ensuring users dont encounter issues only after submitting the form.
    • Include only the Submit button to avoid accidental form resets, which can lead to frustration and abandonment.

User testing provided invaluable insights into several key areas, including:

Usability

Identifying any obstacles or confusing elements that users could encounter, allowing the team to improve and adjustment the overall ease of use.

Efficiency

By measuring the time it took for users to complete specific tasks, enabled optimisation updates to fine tune the the task flow by reducing any unnecessary steps.

Engagement

Helped us understand how users interacted with various elements of the website, such as buttons, links, and forms. This ensured that these components were engaging and intuitive.

Accessibility

Assessing how well the prototype accommodated users with different needs and preferences was imperative, as ensuring the designs was inclusive and accessible to all, decreased the possibility of any negative emotions once developed.

Emotional Response

Capturing the users' emotional reactions during testing the deisgns, helped in gauging User Satisfaction early, identifing areas for enhancing the overall experience, prior to development.

These insights allowed us to make informed design decisions and refine the prototype to better meet the needs and expectations of our users.

Usability Testing Overview

Usability testing is essential for evaluating how effectively and efficiently users can interact with a product's interface. For this purpose, the System Usability Scale (SUS) is a widely recognized and dependable tool. Here's a breakdown of our testing process and results:

System Usability Scale (SUS)

  • Purpose: Measures overall usability through a 10-item questionnaire.
  • Rating: Responses are rated on a 5-point Likert scale.
  • Score Calculation: Results are transformed into a SUS score ranging from 0 to 100.

Testing Process

  • Participants: A group of 5 individuals tested the design.
  • Procedure: Participants followed a specific task flow under controlled conditions.

Final Reflections

This UX case study gave me invaluable insights into the intricate processes of design, research, testing, and iteration. As the sole UX Designer, UX Test Facilitator, UI Designer, and Web Developer for this project, I encountered several challenges. These challenges were expected, and they ultimately expanded my skill set. The key challenges were:

  • Financial Constraints: A limited budget made it impossible to hire additional support, pushing me to stretch my abilities and develop new skills to meet the demands of the project.
  • Time Constraints: My personal tight schedule, combined with the charity's compressed timeline for design, testing, development, and launch, put considerable pressure on me. This resulted in stress and occasional burnout. However, seeing users enjoy the final design made the effort worthwhile.
  • Lack of Team Support: Working alone as both designer and developer meant facing these challenges without a team to rely on for support or feedback.

Despite these obstacles, I remained focused on delivering a solution that balanced user needs with business objectives. Through perseverance, empathy, and a user-centered approach, I created a data-driven design that successfully met the projects goals.

Reflections on the Testing Process

While the System Usability Scale (SUS) provided valuable feedback on the design's effectiveness, I recognize that additional testing methods could have provided even deeper insights. Some of the methods that could have enhanced the evaluation include:

  • Usability Testing: Observing real users interact with the product in real-world settings could have uncovered further usability insights.
  • Heuristic Evaluation: Reviewing the design against established usability heuristics would have helped to identify potential usability issues earlier in the process.

Although time and financial limitations restricted the depth of testing, the tests conducted were sufficient for this particular business case. Nevertheless, a broader mix of UX testing methods could have further strengthened the overall validation of the design.
