Project Introduction

Background

Come Together Hub Ltd is a charity based in St Helens, Merseyside, devoted to supporting vulnerable individuals in the community. With a mission to foster a safe and compassionate environment, the organisation empowers vulnerable members of society through community activities, outreach programmes, and holistic support services.

Before my involvement, the CEO revealed that they had previously hired a Web Developer to create a website intended to enhance their online presence and attract future investments to sustain their critical services. Unfortunately, the website fell short of its objectives due to poor user experience, development shortcomings, lack of mobile optimisation, and slow load times. These issues hindered its effectiveness in showcasing the vital work carried out by the charity.

Problem

Upon thorough evaluation of the existing website, it became evident that despite good intentions, the site faced significant issues. Built on an outdated and unsupported version of WordPress, it remained incomplete and lacked proper functionality.

Further investigation revealed multiple WordPress-related challenges, including file bottlenecks, obsolete jQuery and PHP libraries, and JavaScript vulnerabilities that posed serious security risks. In some cases, the front-end failed to render entirely. Additionally, broken and missing code—such as incomplete functions, non-functional links, and misconfigured scripts—severely impacted performance.

The situation was exacerbated by conflicting CSS rules and media queries, resulting in an unresponsive site. This inconsistency created a frustrating and subpar user experience across all devices.

Solution

After carefully evaluating the challenges of the original website, it was obvious that attempting to repair it would be both impractical and costly. Given that Come Together Hub was in its early stages and operating on a limited budget, a fresh approach was the most viable option.

Inspired by the organisation's dedication and vision, I offered to completely redesign and redevelop their website pro-bono . I believe that a charity as passionate as Come Together Hub deserved a platform that accurately reflected its mission and values, and I was committed to delivering a solution that met those standards.

Research

Creating the charity’s website was a collaborative effort to attract investors and engage the public. The goal was to effectively showcase the organisation’s mission, events, and fundraising opportunities. To achieve this, we conducted market and user research, analysing similar sites, industry trends, and user expectations. By aligning with the charity’s vision, we identified key motivations and challenges, ensuring the user experience featured intuitive navigation and a clear journey that balanced the charity’s goals with audience needs.

Initial Assumptions

Based on professional experience and team discussions, we assumed users would prioritise straightforward navigation, leveraging F and Z-pattern design for content accessibility. Highlighting unique offerings the charity has to offer, such as free therapy sessions and a podcast channel, we decided to include a video streaming feature. This addition aimed to boost engagement, personalise the experience, and strengthen the charity’s online presence through subscriptions and integration with social media and YouTube.

Research 1.1

Understanding the Industry Landscape

To design the Come Together Hub website, we analysed the charity sector to identify trends, best practices, and opportunities for innovation. This ensured alignment with industry standards while addressing the charity’s specific needs.

  • Initial Research: Reviewed successful charity websites to identify features that engage users and encourage donations.
  • Comparative Analysis: Most sites incorporated prominent Donate buttons in their Hero sections to simplify locating and make giving efficient.

By combining industry standards with a customised approach, the website enhances Come Together Hub’s online presence and audience engagement.

Information Architecture

Information architecture (IA) is essential in UX design, organising content to facilitate intuitive and efficient user navigation. IA helps users find information easily by structuring and categorising content logically. This section covers the IA of the application, built on a foundation of user needs, business requirements, and common patterns from similar platforms.

For this project, informed decisions regarding the platform's Information Architecture (IA) were essential in creating a clean, user-friendly structure. By leveraging my expertise in semantic HTML5 , CSS3 , and modular JavaScript (JS/ES5) , I delivered a robust experience within the project’s budget constraints, eliminating the need for a full development team and saving the business thousands of pounds. Assuming the role of Webmaster allowed me to focus on optimising the user journey while ensuring the website met WCAG compliance standards, followed design specifications, was mobile-first responsive, and SEO-friendly.

Key elements of the IA strategy included system navigation and site maps, both of which were integral to shaping the platform’s logical structure and user flow. These elements ensured that the site’s architecture was aligned with both user needs and business goals, contributing to an efficient and intuitive navigation experience.

  • System Navigation: Designed to guide users seamlessly through the platform, ensuring intuitive interactions and minimal cognitive load. This approach maximised efficiency, enabling users to achieve their goals swiftly and supporting key business objectives, such as increasing conversions and aligning with marketing strategies.
  • Site Maps: The site map offers a clear and organised structure of the platform, outlining key sections and their relationships. It enables intuitive navigation, helping users find their way around quickly, while supporting the site’s overall architecture and business objectives.

User Feedback

During the refinement phase, user engagement saw a notable increase, particularly after aligning the marketing strategies on the Landing/Index Page with stakeholder feedback. This alignment was crucial in improving user satisfaction and ensuring the site resonated more with the target audience. Key updates based on direct user input included:

Primary Banner Enhancements

We addressed a significant user pain point by integrating Donation and Recruitment sections into the primary banner on each page. This decision was driven by the need to avoid the intrusive, guilt-driven marketing tactics often seen on charity websites. Users had expressed frustration with the overbearing nature of these approaches, so our goal was to provide clear, accessible options without overwhelming the experience. The response to this change during user testing was overwhelmingly positive, as it allowed users to engage with these key sections immediately, without distraction.

Card Layout Options

Feedback from users on various card layouts was invaluable in guiding design improvements. Key steps taken based on their insights included:

  • Feedback Collection
    We conducted interviews and surveys to gather user preferences on card layouts, assessing which designs felt most intuitive and visually appealing.
  • Final Decision
    The final decision on card layout was postponed to allow for additional refinement. This ensured that we were developing a design that truly reflected user needs and preferences.
  • Low-Fidelity Designs
    Using Figma™, we created several user-centric designs that focused on simplicity, accessibility, and clarity. This enabled us to test the concepts before committing to the final layout.

These refinements aimed not only to improve accessibility but also to ensure that the design aligned with user expectations, ultimately creating a smoother and more enjoyable experience. By incorporating user feedback into every step, we ensured the final design was as effective and user-friendly as possible.

UX Testing 1.1

System Usability Scale (SUS)

Usability testing plays a critical role in evaluating the ease with which users interact with a product. For this project, the System Usability Scale (SUS) was employed as a key metric to assess overall user satisfaction and usability. The SUS survey consists of 10 questions designed to gauge users' perceptions of the product's functionality and ease of use. Using SUS we included several other tests (Task Based and First Click Testing) to save time and ensure results were accurate.

SUS Overview

  • Purpose : Measures overall usability with a 10-question survey.
  • Rating : Responses are rated on a 5-point scale.
  • Score Calculation : Scores range from 0 to 100 based on the answers.

UX Testing: 1.2

Task Design and Question Strategy

Task 1: Navigation

Objective: To evaluate the ease of navigating the system.

Instructions

  1. From the Home page, navigate to the About Us page, in your usual way.
  2. Explore the About Us page, locate and select the link for their See Services page.
  3. On the See Services page, locate and select the link to their Volunteering page.
  4. Explore the content of this page, once you have arrived at the form, return intuitively to the Home page.

Follow-Up Questions:

  • Ease of Use: On a scale of 1-10, 10 being easy, how difficult was it to access and use the main navigation?
  • Alternative Navigation: How difficult was it to find the See Services or similar links?
  • Issues Encountered: Did you face any problems or frustrations during navigation? If so, please describe them.
  • Suggestions for Improvement: Do you have any suggestions to improve the platforms navigation?

Task 2: Video Streaming Service Functionality

Objective: To assess the usability of the video streaming service.

Instructions

  1. From the Home page, navigate to the Podcast page, in your usual way.
  2. Explore the contents, locate and select any video from the right hand side.
  3. Select another video to place in the cue.
  4. Share the video to whichever social media channel you would like.
  5. Return intuitively to the Home page.

Follow-Up Questions:

  • Ease of Use: How easy or difficult was it to play and select the media services?
  • Media Interaction: Were the selected media services moving up the right hand side column when selected?
  • Issues Encountered: Did you face any problems or frustrations during media streaming interaction? If so, please describe them.
  • Suggestions for Improvement: Do you have any suggestions to improve the media streaming service?

Task 3: Intuitive Navigation

Objective: To evaluate the usability of the form.

Instructions:

  1. Navigate to the Volunteer page.
  2. Explore the page and locate the Contact Us form.
  3. Interact with the form, complete the fields incorrectly.
  4. Rectify you mistakes but Do Not press Send.
  5. Intuitively make your way back to home page.

Follow-Up Questions:

  • Ease of Use: How was navigating to this page ?
  • Intuitiveness: Did you find completing the form simple or overwhelming?
  • Issues Encountered: Did you face any problems or frustrations during this task? If so, please describe them.
  • Suggestions for Improvement: Do you have any suggestions to improve the forms functionality?

Results and Analysis

The System Usability Scale (SUS) assessment yielded an impressive score of 90.5 out of 100, significantly exceeding the benchmark of 68. This outstanding score underscores the user-centric effectiveness of the design, indicating that it resonates well with the target audience and affirming its overall usability.

Task 1 - Navigation Ease and Efficiency

This task assessed website usability by instructing users to explore pages via primary and secondary links, starting from the Home screen. With a 6-minute time limit and a pre-selected product, the objective was to evaluate whether navigation was intuitive and efficient.

  • Results:
  • All participants completed the task successfully.
  • 72% of participants finished within the time limit.
  • 28% took up to 6 minutes to complete the task.
  • No participants encountered navigational issues.

Task 2 - Video Streaming Service Functionality

This task evaluated the usability of the Video Streaming Service. Participants navigated from the Home page to the Podcast page, explored its content, and selected videos from the list to ensure the service met user expectations.
From a business perspective, the goal was to validate navigation ease and familiarity to improve usability and ROI. For users, the aim was to deliver a simple, intuitive, and engaging experience to encourage repeat visits.

  • Results:
  • All participants successfully completed the task.
  • 100% engaged with the Podcast streaming service and confirmed functionality met expectations.
  • 12% found some text in the list too small to read.
  • 88% suggested adding more description to the main video.
  • Feedback identified opportunities for improvement.

Task 3 - Independent Navigation and Form Validations

This task evaluated the simplicity and intuitiveness of navigation and form functionality. Participants independently navigated from the Home page to the Volunteer page, explored its content, and engaged with the form fields.

They were instructed to complete the fields incorrectly, observe the progressive disclosures and error handling, and then rectify their mistakes.

From a business perspective, the goal was to ensure the form was functional, simple, and provided clear validation, enabling an efficient user flow that could maximise successful contact submissions.
From a user perspective, the system aimed to minimise cognitive overload and frustration during form completion, delivering an intuitive and engaging experience.

  • Results:
  • All participants successfully completed the task.
  • 100% independently navigated to the Volunteer page.
  • 90% found the form simple to use and appreciated the error handling and progressive disclosures.
  • 10% required additional time to rectify their mistakes.
  • The test revealed valuable opportunities for improvement.

Conclusion

Overall Reflection

This UX case study offered invaluable insights into design, research, testing, and iteration processes. As the sole UX/UI Designer, Test Facilitator, and Web Master, I navigated numerous challenges that ultimately expanded my skill set and knowledge. The key obstacles faced included:

Financial Constraints : With a limited budget, I was responsible for all tasks, which pushed me to quickly adapt and learn to meet project demands.

Time Constraints : A tight personal schedule, alongside the charity's compressed timeline, created significant pressure, occasionally leading to stress. However, the positive user feedback made it worthwhile.

Lack of Team Support : Working solo as both designer and developer meant no immediate support or feedback, which made problem-solving and decision-making more challenging.

Despite these obstacles, I remained committed to delivering a design that balanced user needs with business objectives. My perseverance and user-centred approach led to the creation of a data-driven solution that effectively met the project goals.

Key Achievements

Despite the challenges, the project achieved several significant successes:

  • Secured National Lottery Funding : The project contributed to securing national lottery funding, accelerating further development and future-proofing the initiative.
  • System Usability Scale (SUS) Score : The design received an exceptional SUS score of 90.5/100, far exceeding the industry benchmark of 68, reflecting its effectiveness and user satisfaction.
  • Target Audience Alignment : The design resonated well with the target audience, resulting in high engagement and positive feedback.
  • User Feedback : Participants praised the intuitive interface, highlighting ease of navigation and overall usability.
  • Positive Long-Term Potential : The design laid the groundwork for future growth, offering opportunities for further community benefit.

Process & Testing Reflection

While the System Usability Scale (SUS) provided valuable insights, additional testing methods could have yielded deeper insights. Methods that might have enhanced the evaluation process include:

  • Usability Testing : Observing real users interacting with the product in natural environments could have uncovered additional usability issues.
  • Heuristic Evaluation : A formal review against usability heuristics could have identified design flaws earlier.

While time and budget constraints limited the scope of testing, the methods used were adequate. A broader mix of UX testing techniques would have further validated and strengthened the design.
