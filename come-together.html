Project Introduction

Background

Come Together Hub Ltd is a charitable organization in St Helens, Merseyside, UK, dedicated to offering floating support services for vulnerable individuals. Their mission is to foster a safe and empowering environment where people can improve their lives through positive community activities, outreach programs, and comprehensive support.

They partnered with a web developer to build their website. The goal was to attract more investments and strengthen their online brand awareness, ensuring that they could continue to provide their exceptional services. Unfortunately this website lacked finesse, was not responsive and had an unacceptable page load time.

Problem

After a thorough review to identify to identify and address website deficiencies, it was discovered that, despite good intentions, the site was incomplete and had been developed using an unmaintained and outdated instance of WordPress.

Further investigation revealed several WordPress-related issues, including severe file bottlenecks, JavaScript libraries with security vulnerabilities, and outdated jQuery and PHP libraries. These problems caused the front-end to fail to render in certain areas. Additionally, there was missing or broken code, such as incomplete functions, broken links, and improperly configured scripts, which further hindered the site's performance and functionality. Furthermore, conflicting CSS and erroneous media queries resulted in an unresponsive and unsatisfactory user experience across all devices tested.

Solution

Following a comprehensive assessment of the underlying issues with the original website, and after carefully considering the feasibility of rectification versus the design and development of a new site, it was determined that, given the organization's early stage and limited financial resources, a new approach was needed. Recognizing the potential impact of providing support to such a dedicated and passionate charitable organization, I made the decision to offer to complete the redesign and redevelopment pro-bono. I believed that an organization with the dedication and determination displayed by Come Together Hub deserved a website that matched its values and commitment.

Research

Introduction

In developing this charity's website to attract potential investors and engage the public, our primary goal was to effectively showcase the organization's objectives, upcoming events, and fundraising opportunities. We conducted thorough market and user research to gain deep insights into similar websites, industry dynamics, and user expectations within the charitable sector. By understanding the motivations, pain points, and preferences of stakeholders in the charity sector, we designed a user experience featuring intuitive navigation and an optimized user journey. This approach allowed us to create a platform that is finely tuned to meet both business and user needs.

Initial Assumptions

Based on my experience and expertise, initial assumptions included that users would benefit from a streamlined navigation system, comprehensive content offerings, and layouts following F and Z-pattern principles. The organizations provision of complimentary therapy sessions and a dedicated podcast channel were noted. As a result, the decision to incorporate a video streaming service into the websites design prevailed. This addition aims to enhance user engagement and accessibility, create a unique and personal experience, and strengthen the organization's online presence through subscription options and organic social media and YouTube links.

Research 1.1: Market Research & Competitive Analysis

Understanding the Industry Landscape

  • Initial Research: We conducted a thorough analysis to understand the structure and norms of online charity organizations.
  • Comparative Analysis: We examined key features across various platforms to identify common challenges and opportunities. We found that many charity websites prominently feature Hero and Donate sections on their home pages for a streamlined donation experience.
  • Hero Image Sections: Most charity websites include hero image sections on each page to highlight page title, their mission, donation section, sponsorships, and support links placed above the fold for better visibility.
  • Fundraising Methods: We observed differences between smaller and larger organizations:
    • Smaller organizations often used platforms like "Go Fund Me" due to their own limited resources.
    • Larger organizations preferred to use traditional bank transfers and secure online payment methods.
  • Client-Specific Approach: For Come Together Hub, we initially integrated their existing "Go Fund Me" page for donations, with plans to explore alternative donation methods in the future.
  • Footer Content: Essential content such as contact information, FAQs, social media links, and sponsor affiliations is consistently included in Website footers.
  • Unique Features: Given the clients extensive community services and unique podcast, we aimed to combine the established charity website web standards with a custom, user-friendly design.

Research 1.2: User Interviews & Surveys

Understanding User Expectations

  • User Research: We conducted interviews and online surveys to explore user expectations for Charity Organization Web Services.
  • Diverse Participants: Engaged a diverse group of users from various backgrounds and age demographics to gather a broad range of insights.
  • Objective: Uncovered valuable insights into user needs, pain points, and preferences when navigating charity websites.
  • Insights Gained: Identified user behaviors, sentiments, and frustrations, validating our assumptions with firsthand accounts of past online experiences.
  • Outcome: These insights provided a deep understanding of the challenges and opportunities in creating an effective online platform, guiding our design and development to ensure a user-centric, seamless, and satisfying experience.

Insights from User Research: Key Findings and Analysis

Information Architecture

Structuring for Intuitive Navigation

Information Architecture (IA) is a fundamental component of UX design that focuses on organizing and structuring content to facilitate intuitive navigation and user interaction. Effective IA involves categorizing information, arranging it logically, and designing navigation systems that support user goals and tasks. This section explores the IA of the website, developed based on user needs, business requirements, and best practices observed in similar applications.

Front-End Technology Analysis

Given the client's limited budget for this pro-bono project, hiring a full development team was not financially feasible. Usability Testing

Ensuring the usability and effectiveness of our designs through user experience testing, helped position us for the best possible UX before moving into development.

System Usability Scale (SUS)

Usability testing is a critical process for evaluating the effectiveness and efficiency of a product's user interface. The System Usability Scale (SUS) is a widely used and reliable tool for measuring system usability. It comprises a 10-item questionnaire with responses rated on a 5-point Likert scale, which is then transformed into a SUS score ranging from 0 to 100.

SUS Scores.

To obtain these valuable insights, a group of 5 participants rigorously tested the design following the prescribed task flow under controlled test conditions. As the test facilitator, I facilitated the online testing sessions utilizing Microsoft Teams as a robust platform for scrutinizing users' paralinguistic cues while they navigated through the prototype. Following the completion of the tasks unaided, all participants were requested to independently complete a multiple-choice System Usability Scale (SUS) to gauge the overall usability of the design.

Upon meticulous calculation and analysis, we are pleased to report that our design surpassed the esteemed minimal benchmark of 68/100, achieving an outstanding SUS score of 87.5/100. This resounding success unequivocally signals a user-centric design that resonates with our audience, fueling confidence in the superior quality of our product. We invite you to peruse the accompanying visuals, which include the questionnaire and the impressive final score, underscoring the unequivocal excellence of our design.

After Thoughts

This UX case study has provided me with a comprehensive understanding of design, research, testing, and constant iteration processes. As the sole UX Designer, UX Test Facilitator, and UI Designer at this company, I encountered challenges related to financial struggles, unrealistic time constraints, and a lack of team support. However, these obstacles served as a test of my mettle, and through determination, empathy, and careful considerations to strike the right balance between user and business needs, I successfully delivered this User-Centric and Data-Driven design.

Given the time constraints and budgetary limitations, although the SUS Testing provided valuable insights, I would have preferred to facilitate other complementary testing methodologies to triangulate the data more comprehensively. For instance, usability testing, A/B testing, and heuristic evaluation are additional examples of methodologies that could have further enriched the insights and validation of the design decisions.
