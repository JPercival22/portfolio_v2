Project Introduction

Background

Just Speak Guitar Lessons is an innovative website featuring a non-AI speech recognition algorithm for intuitive and efficient navigation. Inspired by insights gained from a professional UX Designer event hosted by Tech Circus , this project explores advancements in speech recognition technology to enhance accessibility and user engagement. Developed as part of my dissertation, this project earned me a First Class (Hons) BSc in Web Design and Development, demonstrating my dedication to creating inclusive digital experiences.

Problem

While countless guitar tutorials are available online, the challenge is to develop a feature that truly differentiates the platform. This feature must enhance user engagement, simplify learning, and address accessibility needs. A key focus is implementing hands-free functionality, enabling users to navigate tutorials effortlessly—ideal for those with mobility challenges or accessibility requirements. By meeting these needs, the goal is to create an inclusive platform that empowers learners and delivers a more enjoyable and effective guitar learning experience.

Solution

The solution was developed by gaining a deep understanding of user behaviour and using a design sprint approach that focused on continuous feedback. This allowed us to quickly develop and refine ideas, ensuring user needs were considered from the start. By immersing ourselves in the experiences of both beginner and advanced guitar learners, we uncovered valuable insights into their frustrations, motivations, and learning preferences. This user-centred approach helped us shape the platform to suit a diverse range of learners.

Throughout the design process, we carried out regular peer reviews and user testing to maintain quality and improve the platform. Feedback from these sessions played a crucial role in refining the interface and enhancing the overall experience. A key feature of the solution was the introduction of voice command navigation, making it easier to use and reducing cognitive load—especially for beginners who might find traditional interfaces overwhelming.

Overarching Aim : The primary goal was to assess the feasibility of designing and developing a voice-navigable, content-rich website that could operate effectively without relying on AI algorithms. The challenge was to create an intuitive, responsive platform that leveraged voice commands to enhance accessibility and ease of use, while ensuring that the content remained comprehensive, engaging, and easy to digest for users at all skill levels.

Research

Voice Recognition Development

Designing a voice-command tutorial platform for aspiring guitarists focused on our established client base as the target demographic. We prioritised comprehensive user research to uncover their unique needs, preferences, and online behaviours. By understanding their motivations, pain points, and expectations of existing tutorials, we aimed to create a seamless navigation experience and an optimised learning journey.

Initial Assumptions

Based on personal experience as a guitarist, an initial hypothesis suggested that users would benefit significantly from a streamlined navigation system, intuitive placement of key pages and controls within an accessible prompt system, high-resolution media, and detailed content.

Research 1.1

Competitive Analysis and Market Research

Comprehensive research was undertaken at the start of the design process to evaluate the structure and standards of online guitar tutorials. Through detailed comparisons of key features, the aim was to uncover common shortcomings. The research revealed that many platforms offered only basic, low-quality lessons without premium access. Additionally, the content often used language tailored to advanced learners, causing cognitive overload and impeding progress for beginners. These findings highlighted the potential for voice command navigation to address these challenges and improve the learning experience.

Initial Hypothesis

From my personal experience through using tutorial websites, was the assumption was that users may not request a premium access section in the website, this might discourage beginners from continuing their learning journey. User interviews and data synthesis validated this hypothesis, emphasising the need for a more accessible and inclusive solution.

Key Findings from Competitive Analysis

At the start of the design process, detailed research was conducted to evaluate the structure and standards of online guitar tutorials. By analysing and comparing key features, several challenges were identified. Many tutorials offered only basic, low-quality lessons without premium access. Additionally, content often targeted advanced learners, creating cognitive overload and impeding beginners' progress. These challenges underscored the potential of introducing voice command navigation as an innovative solution.

The competitive analysis revealed several critical insights:

  • Lack of Voice Command Navigation: No existing guitar tutorial website featured voice command navigation. This absence presented a significant opportunity for innovation.
  • User Interviews: To validate this opportunity, user interviews were conducted to evaluate the potential usefulness, usability, and desirability of voice command navigation. The interviews explored:
    • User interactions with current online tutorials
    • Opinions on hands-free navigation
    • Specific needs or preferences for voice commands
  • User Engagement: Initial feedback highlighted a strong demand for accessibility-enhancing features, especially for learners who faced difficulties with traditional navigation methods.

These insights formed the foundation for subsequent research and design phases, ensuring that user needs remained central to the development process.

Research 1.2

Understanding User Expectations

Interviews, surveys, and Affinity Mapping revealed key user behaviours, preferences, and frustrations with charity websites. These insights shaped a design strategy focused on delivering an intuitive, engaging experience for the platform.

User research was conducted over two phases, involving interviews with five randomly selected participants (four male, one female) aged 18–22. The sample size adhered to usability research best practices recommended by the Nielsen Norman Group , which evaluated that five participants can uncover most usability issues. Among the participants, three were experienced guitarists using online tutorials regularly, while two were complete beginners.

  • User Research: In-depth interviews and online surveys were conducted to understand user expectations and needs for guitar tutorial platforms, focusing on key features, ease of use, and overall experience.
  • Diverse Participants: Participants were selected from various backgrounds, age groups, and skill levels to capture a broad spectrum of perspectives.
  • Objective: The research aimed to identify user behaviours, pain points, and preferences, providing insights into how users interact with guitar tutorial platforms and what they value most.
  • Insights Gained: The research validated many initial assumptions and revealed user frustrations with existing platforms. It also highlighted features that users found engaging or confusing.
  • Affinity Mapping: An Affinity Map was created to organise data into themes and categories, allowing patterns to emerge. This process prioritised key areas for improvement and innovation.
  • Outcome: The insights and affinity mapping provided a comprehensive understanding of user challenges. These findings directly influenced the design decisions, ensuring a more intuitive and satisfying user experience.

Insights from User Research

The research revealed several important insights about user preferences and experiences:

  • Device Preference: Participants favoured mobile devices as their primary means of interacting with online products.
  • Voice-Navigable Feature: All participants agreed that a voice-navigable feature would enhance their learning experience by eliminating the need for physical actions, such as moving a cursor or turning pages.
  • Pain Points: Experienced players expressed frustration with the requirement for premium access to basic lessons, leading to abandonment of their learning efforts.

Visuals

Participants were enthusiastic about the experience of voice command navigation, appreciating the seamless, hands-free interaction. The addition of engaging visual elements also enhanced user engagement and ease of use.

  • Competitive Pricing: Participants emphasised the importance of affordable pricing structures.
  • Clear Language: Simple, easy-to-understand language was preferred.
  • Visual Content: A preference for prioritising visual content over videos to create a user-friendly environment was clear.

Overview

Participants highlighted shortcomings in speech recognition platforms, particularly in recognising regional accents and idioms, underscoring the need for greater inclusivity. They also noted that complex tutorials and inaccessible language heightened the learning curve, often leading to user drop-off. There was a clear preference for beginner-friendly tutorials with a gradual progression, ensuring accessibility for all skill levels and encouraging long-term engagement.

Interaction Design 1.2

Speech Recognition Technology Evaluation

Extensive research was conducted to find a suitable front-end JavaScript library for integrating speech recognition into the application. Since AI wasn't used, the solution involved manual manipulation of the system's dictionary to align with natural language patterns identified during user research.

To ensure navigation commands felt intuitive, I conducted online surveys, focusing on sourcing a lightweight, efficient, and reliable library to support these custom prompts. With my background in semantic HTML and CSS, but limited JavaScript expertise, I sought a tool with an accessible learning curve for effective implementation.

Identified Libraries:

  1. Artyom
  2. Annyang
  3. Howler
  4. Google Speech

Research Findings

An evaluation of speech recognition libraries—including Artyom, Howler, and Google Speech API—revealed that while widely used, they presented a steep learning curve. With a tight project timeline and sole responsibility for design, development, and testing, a more accessible solution was needed.

After extensive testing, Annyang.js was chosen for its lightweight build, independence from AI, and superior speed and accuracy. The key advantages found are:

  • Compact Size : Annyang’s small footprint improves page load times and reduces network strain.
  • AI-Free Simplicity : Its streamlined algorithm ensures responsive and accurate speech recognition without AI.
  • Developer Availability : A broad pool of developers experienced with Annyang makes it easy to find affordable expertise.
  • Gentle Learning Curve : Annyang’s simplicity makes it easy to implement, even with limited JavaScript experience.
  • Fast Recognition Speed : Annyang outperforms competitors with consistently fast recognition speeds.
  • Strong Community Support : Its active community ensures continuous improvements, updates, and robust support.
  • Cross-Platform Compatibility : Annyang works seamlessly across both Android and iOS, ensuring broad user compatibility.

Low-Fidelity Designs 1.4

A/B Testing

An Online Tuner emerged as a key feature during usability testing, designed to enhance user experience, streamline navigation, and increase engagement.

Option A : Provides audio playback for each guitar string, enabling users to manually tune their instrument to match the standard notes. Option B : Utilises a microphone-based CTA, allowing the system to detect played notes, identify discrepancies, and guide users to correct tuning.

Testing focused on identifying the most intuitive and effective approach to meet user needs and optimise interaction flow.

Option A

Audio-Assisted Tuning

Option A provides audio playback for string notes, blending traditional tuning with voice commands. While effective, it could lead to frustration if guitar sounds overshadow voice commands, preventing the system from recognising inputs.

Test A

Option A. Sound bite Guitar tuner prototype.

Option B

Mic-Based Tuner

Option B uses the device's microphone to detect and display the user's played note through a chromatic scale. While it offers a simpler, more direct tuning experience, the reliance on a third-party library could conflict with the Speech Recognition system, potentially causing technical challenges.

Test B

Microphone Tuner prototype.

Outcomes

Testing with five participants showed that both designs were highly usable, though each presented unique challenges. The tied results indicated their effectiveness, leading to their combined integration in the final product to improve usability and reduce the guitar tuning learning curve.

This feedback underscored the strengths of each design, prompting a balanced integration to maximise their advantages and deliver a comprehensive user experience.

UX Testing

The UX testing phase used the Think Aloud Protocol to gather insights into the prototype’s usability. Participants verbalised their thoughts while interacting with the design, providing valuable feedback for refinements.

UX Testing 1.1

The Think Aloud Protocol involves participants vocalising their thoughts during product use, offering real-time insights into user behaviour. For our Voice-Controlled Guitar Tutorial prototype, it highlighted key areas for improvement.

In line with ethical guidelines, all personal data remains confidential—no recordings or screenshots are included.

Participants navigated the prototype while expressing their thoughts during the session.

Think Aloud Protocol Overview

  • Real-Time User Insights : Participants verbalise their thoughts during tasks, offering immediate insight into decision-making, frustrations, and expectations.
  • Identifies Usability Issues : Reveals pain points, cognitive load, and navigation challenges that may be missed through observation alone.
  • Unfiltered Feedback : As users speak while interacting, the feedback is more natural and immediate, reducing post-test bias or misinterpretation.

UX Testing: 1.2

Task Design and Question Strategy

Task 1: Navigation

Objective: To evaluate the ease of navigating the rich content website using a non-AI voice-controlled system.

Instructions

  1. From any page, locate and select the button to open the Voice Command Hub using any method.
  2. Use a voice command to navigate to any of the Basics lessons.
  3. Use voice commands to scroll up and down the page.
  4. Use a voice command to open "The Rhythm" lesson.
  5. Use a voice command to navigate back to the Home page.

Follow-Up Questions:

  • Ease of Use: On a scale of 1-10, how easy or difficult was it to access and use the Command Hub?
  • Voice Navigation: How intuitive was it to navigate between lessons and content using voice commands?
  • Issues Encountered: Did you experience any difficulties or frustrations during navigation? Please describe them.
  • Suggestions for Improvement: Do you have any suggestions on how the navigation system could be improved?

Task 2: Guitar Tuner Functionality

Objective: To assess the usability of voice commands for the Guitar tuner feature.

Instructions

  1. From the Home page, navigate to the Basics Lesson 1 page, using your preferred method.
  2. Open the Command Hub and navigate to the Tuning Lesson 1 page.
  3. Using the relevant voice command, scroll down the page and select the type of Tuner you'd like to use.
  4. Use a voice command from the Command Hub to select the desired functionality (e.g. play the requested string or start the microphone-assisted tuner).
  5. When you're ready, use voice commands to intuitively return to the Home page.

Follow-Up Questions:

  • Ease of Use: How easy or difficult was it to navigate the page using voice commands?
  • Media Interaction: Did you feel comfortable using voice commands to interact with the Guitar Tuner?
  • Issues Encountered: Did you encounter any difficulties or frustrations when using this feature? Please describe them.
  • Improvement Suggestions: Do you have any suggestions for improving voice control for this feature?

Task 3: Independent Navigation

Objective: To evaluate the intuitiveness of navigation using voice commands for various pages and media.

Instructions:

  1. Use voice commands to navigate to four different pages of your choice.
  2. Browse each page and interact with any media using voice commands.
  3. If unsure about commands, feel free to access the Command Hub at any time.

Conclusion

Overall Reflection

This UX case study provided invaluable experience in design, research, testing, and iteration. As the sole UX/UI Designer, Test Facilitator, and Web Master, I faced several challenges that enhanced my adaptability and problem-solving skills:

  • Financial Constraints : A limited budget required me to handle all aspects of the project, accelerating my learning and efficiency.
  • Time Constraints : Balancing a tight personal schedule with the charity’s timeline was demanding, but positive user feedback justified the effort.
  • Independent Execution : Managing design and development alone meant problem-solving without immediate support, making decision-making more complex.

Despite these challenges, I remained committed to a user-centric approach, delivering a data-driven solution that effectively met project goals.

Key Achievements

  • Exceptional Usability : Achieved a System Usability Scale (SUS) score of 90.5/100, surpassing the industry benchmark of 68.
  • Target Audience Alignment : High engagement and positive feedback confirmed the design’s effectiveness.
  • User Satisfaction : Participants praised intuitive navigation and seamless usability.
  • Scalability : The design established a solid foundation for future enhancements.

Process & Testing Reflection

While the System Usability Scale (SUS) provided valuable insights, additional methods could have further strengthened the evaluation:

  • Usability Testing : Observing users in real-world scenarios could have revealed additional pain points.
  • Heuristic Evaluation : A structured review against usability principles could have identified early design flaws.

Despite budget and time constraints, the chosen methods were effective. A broader testing approach could have further validated the design’s strengths.
