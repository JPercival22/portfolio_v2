Project Introduction

Project Brief, Business Requirements & Problem

Background

Just Speak Guitar Lessons is an intuitive website that utilises a non-AI speech recognition algorithm for seamless navigation. This innovation was inspired by a professional UX Designer meeting hosted by Tech Circus, focusing on advancements in speech recognition technology. By integrating this feature, the website enhances accessibility and boosts user engagement. This project served as my dissertation, contributing to my achievement of obtaining a First Class (Hons) BSc degree in Web Design and Development, reflecting my commitment to creating inclusive digital experiences.

Problem

Although there is an abundance of guitar tutorials available online, each catering to various niches and skill levels, the challenge lies in developing a distinctive feature that truly stands out. This feature must not only captivate users but also enhance their overall engagement and simplify the learning experience. A critical aspect of this solution is the implementation of hands-free functionality, allowing users to navigate the tutorials without needing to use their hands, which is especially beneficial for those who may have mobility challenges or other accessibility issues. By addressing these needs, the aim is to create an inclusive platform that empowers all learners, fostering a more enjoyable and effective guitar learning journey.

Solution

By thoroughly understanding user behaviour and employing a design sprint methodology with regular feedback loops, ideas were rapidly developed and validated. I empathised with both novice learners and experienced users, gaining insights into their frustrations and aspirations. The project also underwent rigorous peer review to ensure its quality and effectiveness. Overarching aim: Yo measure the feasibility of designing and developing a voice-navigable, rich-content website using a non-AI algorithm.

Research

Introduction

In designing a voice command tutorial platform for aspiring guitarists, we focused on our established client base as the target demographic. Our approach prioritised comprehensive user research to gain deep insights into their unique needs, preferences, and online behaviours. By understanding their motivations, pain points, and expectations regarding existing online tutorials, we aimed to create a user experience marked by seamless navigation and an optimised customer journey.

Initial Assumptions

Drawing from my own experiences as a guitar player, an initial Hypothesis emerged: users would greatly benefit from a streamlined navigation system, strategic placement of key pages and controls within an easily accessible prompt system, as well as high-resolution media and detailed content.


Research 1.1: Competitive Analysis and Market Research

Understanding the Industry Landscape

As the design process began, comprehensive research was conducted to understand the structure and standards of online guitar tutorials. By meticulously comparing key features, the goal was to identify prevailing issues. This phase revealed that many tutorials offered basic lessons of substandard quality unless users opted for premium access. Additionally, the language often catered to advanced students, leading to cognitive overload that could hinder the learning curve. In response, the potential for introducing voice command navigation was explored to alleviate these challenges.

Early Hypothesis

An early assumption was that the requirement for premium access might discourage many beginners from continuing their learning journey. Synthesizing data from user interviews confirmed this hypothesis.

A Competitive Research Table highlighting the Market and Competitive Analysis of existing Guitar Tutorial Websites was created, showcasing discovered insights.

Key Findings from Competitive Analysis

The competitive analysis yielded several important insights:

  • Lack of Voice Command Navigation: One of the most striking discoveries was that no existing guitar tutorial website provided a voice command navigation feature. This gap in the market highlighted an opportunity for innovation.
  • User Interviews: To further explore this opportunity, user interviews were conducted to evaluate the potential usefulness, usability, and desirability of integrating voice command navigation into the platform. The interviews aimed to gather feedback on:
    • How users interact with current online tutorials
    • Their thoughts on hands-free navigation
    • Any specific needs or preferences regarding voice commands
  • User Engagement: Initial responses indicated a strong interest in features that enhance accessibility and user engagement, particularly for learners who may struggle with traditional navigation methods.

These findings laid the groundwork for the next phase of research and design, ensuring that user needs and preferences were at the forefront of the development process.


Research 1.2: User Interviews & Surveys

Understanding User Expectations

The user research was conducted in two phases, involving interviews with five participants (four male and one female), aged between 18 and 22 years. This sample size was chosen in accordance with established usability research practices, such as those recommended by Nielsen Norman Group, which indicates that five participants can effectively uncover the majority of usability issues. Among the participants, three were experienced guitar players who regularly used online tutorials, while two were complete beginners eager to learn.

  • User Research: We conducted a series of in-depth interviews and online surveys, aiming to understand user expectations and needs for charity websites, focusing on key features, ease of use, and overall experience.
  • Diverse Participants: We engaged a varied group of users from different backgrounds, age groups, and levels of digital literacy to capture a wide range of perspectives.
  • Objective: Our aim was to uncover important insights into user behaviours, pain points, and preferences. These findings gave us a better understanding of how people interact with charity platforms and what they find most valuable.
  • Insights Gained: Through this research, we identified common user behaviours and emotions, as well as frustrations with existing charity websites. This validated many of our initial assumptions and provided direct input on what users found confusing or engaging.
  • Affinity Mapping: To organise and synthesise the data, we created an Affinity Mapa method used to group related insights into themes or categories. By clustering similar responses together, we were able to spot patterns and prioritise key areas of improvement and innovation for the site. This mapping process guided our decisions for a user-focused design.
  • Outcome: These insights, along with the affinity mapping, gave us a comprehensive understanding of the challenges users face when navigating charity websites. The findings directly informed our design choices, ensuring that the new platform would provide a seamless, intuitive, and satisfying user experience.

Insights from User Research: Key Findings and Analysis

The research revealed several important insights about user preferences and experiences:

  • Device Preference: Participants showed a strong preference for using mobile devices as their primary means of interacting with online products.
  • Voice-Navigable Feature: All participants agreed that a voice-navigable feature would greatly enhance their learning experience by eliminating the need for physical actions, such as moving a cursor or turning pages in physical books.
  • Pain Points: Experienced players shared several frustrations in their learning journeys, particularly dissatisfaction with the requirement for premium access to basic lessons, which often led to abandonment of their efforts.

Visuals

Participants expressed enthusiasm for the experience of online voice command navigation, appreciating the seamless, hands-free interaction it offered. Additionally, the incorporation of engaging visual elements significantly enhanced the overall user experience, leading to increased engagement and ease of use.

Key Information

  • Competitive Pricing: Participants emphasised the importance of affordable pricing structures.
  • Clear Language: They preferred simple, straightforward language that is easy to understand.
  • Visual Content: There was a clear preference for prioritising visual content over videos, which was seen as crucial for creating a user-friendly environment.

Overview

Participants noted limitations in existing speech recognition platforms regarding their ability to understand local accents and idioms. This highlighted the need for greater inclusivity and adaptability to regional language nuances. Additionally, they pointed out challenges associated with advanced tutorials and beginner-unfriendly language, which increased the learning curve and often led to user abandonment. A clear preference emerged for beginner-centric tutorials that offer a gradual learning curve, catering to users at various skill levels and fostering sustained engagement with the application.

Interaction Design 1.2: Speech Recognition Technology Analysis

Extensive research was undertaken to find a front-end JavaScript library suited for integrating speech recognition technology into the application. As AI wasnt being utilised, the solution required manual manipulation of the systems dictionary to match natural language patterns identified during user research.

To align speech prompts with real-world user input, online surveys were conducted, ensuring that navigation commands felt intuitive. I focused on sourcing a lightweight, efficient, and reliable library that could support these custom prompts while maintaining high performance. With my experience in semantic HTML and CSS, but limited knowledge of JavaScript, selecting a tool with an accessible learning curve was essential for effective implementation.

Identified Libraries:

  1. Artyom
  2. Annyang
  3. Howler
  4. Google Speech

Research Findings

Through a thorough evaluation of various speech recognition libraries, including Artyom, Howler, and Google's Speech API, it became evident that despite their global success, they posed a significant learning curve. Given the tight project timeline, and my sole responsibility for design, development, and testing as part of my Research and Development Dissertation, a more accessible solution was necessary.

After rigorous testing, Annyang.js was chosen for its compact size, lack of AI complexity, and superior performance in speed and accuracy compared to the alternatives.

Key Advantages of Annyang.js

  • Compact Size
    Annyangs small footprint ensures faster page load times, improving user experience by reducing strain on the network.
  • AI-Free Simplicity
    While it doesnt rely on AI, its streamlined algorithm ensures responsive and accurate speech recognition.
  • Developer Availability
    There is a broad pool of developers experienced with Annyang, making it easier to find affordable expertise when needed.
  • Gentle Learning Curve
    Annyang is simple to implement, making it ideal for a developer with limited JavaScript experience.
  • Fast Recognition Speed
    Annyang consistently delivers faster recognition speeds, outperforming its competitors.
  • Strong Community Support
    Its active online community offers continuous improvements, frequent updates, and robust support.
  • Cross-Platform Compatibility
    Annyang operates smoothly across both Android and iOS, ensuring it can be used by a wide range of users.

Low-Fidelity Designs 1.4: Usability Testing

Users expressed a clear need for an Online Tuner. Throughout the design iterations, usability testing was conducted to evaluate which design would enhance user-friendliness, improve navigation flow, and boost conversion rates.

Option A offers audio feedback for each guitar string, enabling learners to match their guitar's tuning to the standard note.
Option B requires the use of a microphone through a call to action (CTA). Once the system detects the sound of the played guitar strings, it identifies the note and indicates whether it is out of tune.

Option A: Audio Tuner

Option A offers enhanced voice commands and tailored prompts for tuning guitar strings. Users can engage with buttons to play sound bites of the notes for selected strings, allowing for both manual and voice interactions during the tuning process. However, the inclusion of these extra steps and voice commands may result in user confusion, increase the learning curve, and potentially lead to frustration.

Test A

Mp4 Guitar Tuner prototype.

Option B: Dynamic Mic Tuner

Option B presents a dynamic solution by leveraging the device's microphone to analyse the user's played note and display it through a chromatic metric. Once the tuner accurately identifies the correct note, an indicator appears. Although this approach relies on a third-party library, which may conflict with the chosen Speech Recognition library, it provides a more straightforward experience for the user.

Test B

Microphone Tuner prototype.

Outcomes

Testing with five participants revealed that both designs offered strong usability, although each presented challenges in the decision-making process. The tied results indicated the effectiveness of both options, leading to their integration in the final product to enhance site usability and reduce the learning curve for guitar tuning.

This feedback highlighted the strengths of each design, emphasising their unique merits. Consequently, a balanced integration of both designs was implemented to leverage their advantages and provide a comprehensive user experience.

UX Testing 1.2: Real-Time Usability Study

The Think Aloud Protocol

The Think Aloud Protocol is a common usability testing method where participants verbalise their thoughts while using a product, offering real-time insights into user behaviour and potential usability issues. For our Voice-Controlled Guitar Tutorial prototype, it highlighted key areas for improvement.

As per ethical guidelines, all personal data remains confidential, so no recordings or screenshots are included.

During a session, participants navigate the prototype and articulate their thoughts. The process typically includes:

  • Briefing: Participants are informed about the session's purpose and instructed to vocalise their thoughts as they interact with the prototype.
  • Task Execution: Participants complete tasks, such as starting lessons or using voice commands.
  • Observation: Facilitators observe and take notes on comments, actions, and difficulties.
  • Follow-Up Questions: Post-session, participants answer questions to clarify feedback.

Pros and Cons

Pros

  • Rich Qualitative Data: Reveals user thought processes, offering insights that typical usability testing may miss.
  • Immediate Feedback: Designers can identify and address issues in real-time, leading to iterative improvements.
  • User Perspectives: Direct feedback helps designers create more user-centric solutions.
  • Uncovers Hidden Issues: Users often voice frustrations or confusion that may not be immediately visible.

Cons

  • Behaviour Influence: Thinking aloud can lead to unnatural interactions.
  • Participant Discomfort: Some may struggle to verbalise their thoughts, affecting data quality.
  • Data Interpretation: Analysing this data can be time-intensive and require careful interpretation.
  • Limited Quantitative Data: Primarily qualitative, this method may need supplementary techniques for comprehensive analysis.

UX Testing 1.3: User Experience Testing

Task Design and Question Strategy

Task 1: Navigation

Objective: To evaluate the ease of navigating the rich content website using a non-AI voice-controlled system.

Instructions

  1. Find the Command Hub: From any page, locate and select the button to open the Voice Command Hub.
  2. Open The Basics Lesson: Use a voice command to navigate to "The Basics" lesson.
  3. Browse The Basics Lesson: Use voice commands to scroll up and down the page.
  4. Navigate to The Rhythm Lesson: Use a voice command to open "The Rhythm" lesson.
  5. Return to the Home Page: Use a voice command to navigate back to the home page.

Follow-Up Questions:

  • Ease of Use: How easy or difficult was it to find and use the Command Hub?
  • Navigation: Were the voice commands intuitive for navigating between lessons and pages?
  • Issues Encountered: Did you face any problems or frustrations during navigation? If so, please describe them.
  • Suggestions for Improvement: Do you have any suggestions to improve the voice control navigation?

Task 2: Media Interaction

Objective: To assess the usability of voice commands for media interaction.

Instructions

  1. Navigate to The Strings Lesson: Use the Command Hub and voice commands to navigate to "The Strings" lesson.
  2. Browse The Strings Lesson: Use voice commands to scroll down the page.
  3. Play and Stop a Video: Use a voice command to select a video by its title and play it. Use a voice command to stop the video.
  4. Explore the Page: Browse the page as you wish using voice commands.
  5. Return to the Home Page: Use a voice command to navigate back to the home page.

Follow-Up Questions:

  • Ease of Use: How easy or difficult was it to play and stop videos using voice commands?
  • Media Interaction: Were the voice commands intuitive for interacting with media?
  • Issues Encountered: Did you face any problems or frustrations during media interaction? If so, please describe them.
  • Suggestions for Improvement: Do you have any suggestions to improve the voice control for media interaction?

Task 3: Intuitive Navigation

Objective: To evaluate the intuitiveness of navigation using voice commands for various pages and media.

Instructions:

  1. Navigate to Four Different Pages: Use the basic commands learned in previous tasks to navigate to four different pages of your choice.
  2. Browse and Interact with Media: Browse each page using voice commands. Play any media or interact with any carousel using voice commands.
  3. Use the Command Hub if Needed: You can view the Command Hub at any point if you are unsure of the commands.
  4. Return to the Home Page: Use a voice command to navigate back to the home page once you are satisfied.

Follow-Up Questions:

  • Ease of Use: How easy or difficult was it to navigate to different pages and interact with media using voice commands?
  • Intuitiveness: Did you find the voice commands intuitive for navigating and interacting with the content?
  • Issues Encountered: Did you face any problems or frustrations during this task? If so, please describe them.
  • Suggestions for Improvement: Do you have any suggestions to improve the overall voice control functionality?
