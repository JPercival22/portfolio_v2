Project Introduction

Background

Just Speak Guitar Lessons is an innovative website featuring a non-AI speech recognition algorithm for intuitive and efficient navigation. Inspired by insights gained from a professional UX Designer event hosted by Tech Circus , this project explores advancements in speech recognition technology to enhance accessibility and user engagement. Developed as part of my dissertation, this project earned me a First Class (Hons) BSc in Web Design and Development, demonstrating my dedication to creating inclusive digital experiences.

Problem

While countless guitar tutorials are available online, the challenge is to develop a feature that truly differentiates the platform. This feature must enhance user engagement, simplify learning, and address accessibility needs. A key focus is implementing hands-free functionality, enabling users to navigate tutorials effortlessly—ideal for those with mobility challenges or accessibility requirements. By meeting these needs, the goal is to create an inclusive platform that empowers learners and delivers a more enjoyable and effective guitar learning experience.

Solution

The solution was developed through a comprehensive understanding of user behaviour, coupled with a design sprint methodology that emphasised iterative feedback loops. This approach enabled the rapid development and validation of ideas in a short time frame, ensuring that user needs were addressed early in the design process. By immersing ourselves in the experiences of both novice and experienced guitar learners, we gained valuable insights into their frustrations, motivations, and learning preferences. This empathy-driven approach allowed us to tailor the platform to meet the specific needs of a diverse user base.

Throughout the design process, regular peer reviews and user testing sessions were conducted to ensure the quality and effectiveness of the platform. Feedback from these sessions was instrumental in refining the user interface and enhancing the overall user experience. A key aspect of the solution was the integration of voice command navigation, designed to simplify the learning process and reduce cognitive overload, particularly for beginners who may struggle with traditional interface-based navigation.

Overarching Aim: The primary goal was to assess the feasibility of designing and developing a voice-navigable, content-rich website that could operate effectively without relying on AI algorithms. The challenge was to create an intuitive, responsive platform that leveraged voice commands to enhance accessibility and ease of use, while ensuring that the content remained comprehensive, engaging, and easy to digest for users at all skill levels.

Research

Designing a voice-command tutorial platform for aspiring guitarists focused on our established client base as the target demographic. We prioritised comprehensive user research to uncover their unique needs, preferences, and online behaviours. By understanding their motivations, pain points, and expectations of existing tutorials, we aimed to create a seamless navigation experience and an optimised learning journey.

Initial Assumptions

Based on personal experience as a guitarist, an initial hypothesis suggested that users would benefit significantly from a streamlined navigation system, intuitive placement of key pages and controls within an accessible prompt system, high-resolution media, and detailed content.


Research 1.1

Competitive Analysis and Market Research

Comprehensive research was undertaken at the start of the design process to evaluate the structure and standards of online guitar tutorials. Through detailed comparisons of key features, the aim was to uncover common shortcomings. The research revealed that many platforms offered only basic, low-quality lessons without premium access. Additionally, the content often used language tailored to advanced learners, causing cognitive overload and impeding progress for beginners. These findings highlighted the potential for voice command navigation to address these challenges and improve the learning experience.

Initial Hypothesis

A key assumption was that requiring premium access would discourage beginners from continuing their learning journey. User interviews and data synthesis validated this hypothesis, emphasising the need for a more accessible and inclusive solution.

Key Findings from Competitive Analysis

At the start of the design process, detailed research was conducted to evaluate the structure and standards of online guitar tutorials. By analysing and comparing key features, several challenges were identified. Many tutorials offered only basic, low-quality lessons without premium access. Additionally, content often targeted advanced learners, creating cognitive overload and impeding beginners' progress.   • Lack of Voice Command Navigation: No existing guitar tutorial website featured voice command navigation. This absence presented a significant opportunity for innovation.
  • User Interviews: To validate this opportunity, user interviews were conducted to evaluate the potential usefulness, usability, and desirability of voice command navigation. The interviews explored:
    • User interactions with current online tutorials
    • Opinions on hands-free navigation

  • User Engagement: Initial feedback highlighted a strong demand for accessibility-enhancing features, especially for learners who faced difficulties with traditional navigation methods.

These insights formed the foundation for subsequent research and design phases, ensuring that user needs remained central to the development process.

Research 1.2: User Interviews & Surveys

Understanding User Expectations

User research was conducted in two phases, involving interviews with five participants (four male, one female) aged 18–22. The sample size adhered to usability research best practices recommended by the Nielsen Norman Group , which suggests that five participants can uncover most usability issues. Among the participants, three were experienced guitarists using online tutorials regularly, while two were complete beginners.

  • User Research: In-depth interviews and online surveys were conducted to understand user expectations and needs for guitar tutorial platforms, focusing on key features, ease of use, and overall experience.
  • Diverse Participants: Participants were selected from various backgrounds, age groups, and skill levels to capture a broad spectrum of perspectives.
  • Objective: The research aimed to identify user behaviours, pain points, and preferences, providing insights into how users interact with guitar tutorial platforms and what they value most.
  • Insights Gained: The research validated many initial assumptions and revealed user frustrations with existing platforms. It also highlighted features that users found engaging or confusing.
  • Affinity Mapping: An Affinity Map was created to organise data into themes and categories, allowing patterns to emerge. This process prioritised key areas for improvement and innovation.
  • Outcome: The insights and affinity mapping provided a comprehensive understanding of user challenges. These findings directly influenced the design decisions, ensuring a more intuitive and satisfying user experience.

Insights from User Research: Key Findings and Analysis

The research revealed several important insights about user preferences and experiences:

  • Device Preference: Participants favoured mobile devices as their primary means of interacting with online products.
  • Voice-Navigable Feature: All participants agreed that a voice-navigable feature would enhance their learning experience by eliminating the need for physical actions, such as moving a cursor or turning pages.
  • Pain Points: Experienced players expressed frustration with the requirement for premium access to basic lessons, leading to abandonment of their learning efforts.

Visuals

Participants were enthusiastic about the experience of voice command navigation, appreciating the seamless, hands-free interaction. The addition of engaging visual elements also enhanced user engagement and ease of use.

Key Information

  • Competitive Pricing: Participants emphasised the importance of affordable pricing structures.
  • Clear Language: Simple, easy-to-understand language was preferred.
  • Visual Content: A preference for prioritising visual content over videos to create a user-friendly environment was clear.

Overview

Participants noted limitations in current speech recognition platforms, particularly in understanding regional accents and idioms. This highlighted the need for more inclusivity and adaptability. They also pointed out the challenges with advanced tutorials and beginner-unfriendly language, which increased the learning curve and often led to user abandonment. A clear preference emerged for beginner-centric tutorials offering a gradual learning curve, supporting users of all skill levels and promoting continued engagement.

Interaction Design 1.2

Speech Recognition Technology Evaluation

Extensive research was conducted to find a suitable front-end JavaScript library for integrating speech recognition into the application. Since AI wasn't used, the solution involved manual manipulation of the system's dictionary to align with natural language patterns identified during user research.

To ensure navigation commands felt intuitive, I conducted online surveys, focusing on sourcing a lightweight, efficient, and reliable library to support these custom prompts. With my background in semantic HTML and CSS, but limited JavaScript expertise, I sought a tool with an accessible learning curve for effective implementation.

Identified Libraries:

  1. Artyom
  2. Annyang
  3. Howler
  4. Google Speech

Research Findings

After evaluating several speech recognition libraries, including Artyom, Howler, and Google Speech API, it became clear that while they were globally successful, they involved a steep learning curve. Given the tight project timeline and my responsibility for design, development, and testing, a more accessible solution was required.

Following extensive testing, Annyang.js was selected for its compact size, non-reliance on AI, and superior speed and accuracy compared to alternatives.

Key Advantages of Annyang.js

  • Compact Size
    Annyang’s small footprint improves page load times and reduces network strain.
  • AI-Free Simplicity
    Its streamlined algorithm ensures responsive and accurate speech recognition without AI.
  • Developer Availability
    A broad pool of developers experienced with Annyang makes it easy to find affordable expertise.
  • Gentle Learning Curve
    Annyang’s simplicity makes it easy to implement, even with limited JavaScript experience.
  • Fast Recognition Speed
    Annyang outperforms competitors with consistently fast recognition speeds.
  • Strong Community Support
    Its active community ensures continuous improvements, updates, and robust support.
  • Cross-Platform Compatibility
    Annyang works seamlessly across both Android and iOS, ensuring broad user compatibility.

Low-Fidelity Designs 1.4

A/B Testing

An Online Tuner emerged as a key feature during usability testing, designed to enhance user experience, streamline navigation, and increase engagement.

Option A : Provides audio playback for each guitar string, enabling users to manually tune their instrument to match the standard notes. Option B : Utilises a microphone-based CTA, allowing the system to detect played notes, identify discrepancies, and guide users to correct tuning.

Testing focused on identifying the most intuitive and effective approach to meet user needs and optimise interaction flow.

Option A

Audio-Assisted Tuning

Option A provides audio playback for string notes, blending traditional tuning with voice commands. While effective, it could lead to frustration if guitar sounds overshadow voice commands, preventing the system from recognising inputs.

Test A

Option A. Sound bite Guitar tuner prototype.

Option B

Mic-Based Tuner

Option B uses the device's microphone to detect and display the user's played note through a chromatic scale. While it offers a simpler, more direct tuning experience, the reliance on a third-party library could conflict with the Speech Recognition system, potentially causing technical challenges.

Test B

Microphone Tuner prototype.

Outcomes

Testing with five participants showed that both designs were highly usable, though each presented unique challenges. The tied results indicated their effectiveness, leading to their combined integration in the final product to improve usability and reduce the guitar tuning learning curve.

This feedback underscored the strengths of each design, prompting a balanced integration to maximise their advantages and deliver a comprehensive user experience.

UX Testing 1.1

The Think Aloud Protocol

The Think Aloud Protocol is a common usability testing method where participants verbalise their thoughts while using a product, offering real-time insights into user behaviour and potential usability issues. For our Voice-Controlled Guitar Tutorial prototype, it highlighted key areas for improvement.

As per ethical guidelines, all personal data remains confidential, so no recordings or screenshots are included.

During a session, participants navigate the prototype and articulate their thoughts. The process typically includes:

  • Briefing:
    Participants are informed about the session's purpose and instructed to vocalise their thoughts as they interact with the prototype.
  • Task Execution:
    Participants complete tasks, such as starting lessons or using voice commands.
  • Observation:
    Facilitators observe and take notes on comments, actions, and difficulties.
  • Follow-Up Questions:
    Post-session, participants answer questions to clarify feedback.

Pros and Cons

Pros

  • Rich Qualitative Data:
    Reveals user thought processes, offering insights that typical usability testing may miss.
  • Immediate Feedback:
    Designers can identify and address issues in real-time, leading to iterative improvements.
  • User Perspectives:
    Direct feedback helps designers create more user-centric solutions.
  • Uncovers Hidden Issues:
    Users often voice frustrations or confusion that may not be immediately visible.

Cons

  • Behaviour Influence:
    Thinking aloud can lead to unnatural interactions.
  • Participant Discomfort:
    Some may struggle to verbalise their thoughts, affecting data quality.
  • Data Interpretation:
    Analysing this data can be time-intensive and require careful interpretation.
  • Limited Quantitative Data:
    Primarily qualitative, this method may need supplementary techniques for comprehensive analysis.

UX Testing 1.2

Task Design and Question Strategy

Task 1: Navigation

Objective: To evaluate the ease of navigating the rich content website using a non-AI voice-controlled system.

Instructions

  1. Find the Command Hub: From any page, locate and select the button to open the Voice Command Hub.
  2. Open The Basics Lesson: Use a voice command to navigate to "The Basics" lesson.
  3. Browse The Basics Lesson: Use voice commands to scroll up and down the page.
  4. Navigate to The Rhythm Lesson: Use a voice command to open "The Rhythm" lesson.
  5. Return to the Home Page: Use a voice command to navigate back to the home page.

Follow-Up Questions:

  • Ease of Use: How easy or difficult was it to find and use the Command Hub?
  • Navigation: Were the voice commands intuitive for navigating between lessons and pages?
  • Issues Encountered: Did you face any problems or frustrations during navigation? If so, please describe them.
  • Suggestions for Improvement: Do you have any suggestions to improve the voice control navigation?

Task 2: Guitar Tuner Interaction

Objective: To assess the usability of voice commands for the Guitar tuner feature.

Instructions

  1. Explore the Home page.
  2. Use the Command Hub to navigate to Tuning lesson .
  3. Use voice commands to scroll down the page and select the Tuner you would like to use.
  4. Use a voice command to select its functionality (e.g) Play the requested string or to start the Microphone assisted tuner.
  5. When you are ready, intuitively return to the Home Page , using the Voice commands

Follow-Up Questions:

  • Ease of Use: How was page navigation using the Voice commands?
  • Media Interaction: Were the voice commands straight forward when interacting with the Guitar Tuner features?
  • Issues Encountered: Did you face any problems or frustrations during interacting with this feature? If so, please describe them.
  • Suggestions for Improvement: Do you have any suggestions to improve the voice control for this feature?

Task 3: Intuitive Navigation

Objective: To evaluate the intuitiveness of navigation using voice commands for various pages and media.

Instructions:

  1. Navigate to Four Different Pages: Use the basic commands learned in previous tasks to navigate to four different pages of your choice.
  2. Browse and Interact with Media: Browse each page using voice commands. Play any media or interact with any carousel using voice commands.
  3. Use the Command Hub if Needed: You can view the Command Hub at any point if you are unsure of the commands.
  4. Return to the Home Page: Use a voice command to navigate back to the home page once you are satisfied.

Follow-Up Questions:

  • Ease of Use: How easy or difficult was it to navigate to different pages and interact with media using voice commands?
  • Intuitiveness: Did you find the voice commands intuitive for navigating and interacting with the content?
  • Issues Encountered: Did you face any problems or frustrations during this task? If so, please describe them.
  • Suggestions for Improvement: Do you have any suggestions to improve the overall voice control functionality?

Results and Analysis

The testing phase involved five participants, all engaging in the task-based evaluation using the Think Aloud Protocol. Each participant completed the tasks while verbalising their thoughts, which provided valuable insights into their navigation process, ease of interaction, and overall experience with the prototype, using the System Usability Scale (SUS).

Task 1 - Navigation Ease and Efficiency

This test evaluated the usability of the voice navigation, from finding the Commands List to the navigating pages and scrolling content. The task was time-based (6 minutes), with pre-selected destinations to simplify the process. The objective was to determine whether navigation was intuitive and straightforward.

  • Results:
  • All participants completed the task successfully.
  • 76% of participants finished within the time limit.
  • 24% took up to 6 minutes to complete the task.
  • No participants encountered navigational issues.

Task 2 - Media interaction

This test assessed the usability of the Guitar Tuning section. Following the same journey as Task 1, with the additional task of selecting which feature that they would like to use.
From a business perspective, the goal was to determine which feature could be eliminated. From a user perspective, the system was designed to provide a simple, intuitive, and engaging experience to improve feature usability.

  • Results:
  • All participants completed the task successfully.

Conclusion

Final Reflections

This UX case study has deepened my understanding of design processes, research methodologies, and the importance of rigorous testing and iteration. Drawing on my experience as a guitar teacher and the feedback from my students, I approached this project with a clear objective.

The primary goal was to create a user-friendly study aid for aspiring guitarists, providing essential tutorial materials without disrupting their practice. By minimising distractions and simplifying the learning process, I aimed to lower the entry barrier for beginners and enhance their overall experience.
