Introduction

Background

Just Speak Guitar Lessons is a vocally navigable website using a non AI speech recognition algorithm. The inspiration came from a professional UX Designer meeting (courtesy of UX Circus Ltd) concerning advancements in Speech recognition and how including this improves Accessability and increases User engagement.

Problem

There are already a number of Guitar Tutorials in the public domain, each with their own niche. The challenge lies in creating a unique feature that the Users want to engage and react with that would improve their engagement and decrease the learning curve.

My Role

UX Researcher, UX & UI Designer Branding, Front End Development and User Testing Facilitator

Solution

By having a deep understanding of user behaviour and applying a design sprint methodology with regular feedback stages, ideas were rapidly developed and validated. Empathise with those who are learning and those who have experience with products already on the market, understand their frustrations and desires.

Research

Market Research & Competitive Analysis

I began the design process by conducting research to better understand the online Guitar Tutorials structure and standards. Key features were also compared to see what problems they presented. Personal discoveries were found, that the majority of the tutorials provided sub-standard basic lessons unless one was willing to pay for premium access, the language was aimed more towards advanced students. Further into the research I wanted to see how the public would react to introducing voice command navigation to help ease cognitive overload and reduce the learning curve, by minimising unnecessary movements.

An early assumption here, was that the access premium, could be the reason that many beginners, decide not to continue with their learning.

Synthesised data from User Interviews confirmed this assumption.

No website offers Voice command navigation so in the next phase User Interviews were conducted to see if this would be Useful, Usable and Desired.

Synthesize

User Interviews

Five participants were interviewed (4 Male and 1 Female) over Two phases. The majority were aged between the ages of 18 and 22 years old. Three participants had played guitar for a while and had a lot of experience using online tutorials and two participants had never played a guitar but had always wanted to learn how to.

Research revealed that participants often prefer to interact with internet products, the general consensus was via a mobile device. All participants agreed that having voice navigable feature would certainly make learning easier. Participants who had previous experience with online tutorials, shared their learning journey behaviors, their thoughts and feelings surrounding learning to play the guitar and their experiences with using guitar tutorials, highlighting pain points and elements they thought should have been there for a beginner. All participants confirmed my initial assumption by agreeing the Premium to access the basic of lessons put them off and in most cases caused abandonment.

Below is the affinity map following these interviews, click on the image to zoom in.

Outcomes

1. Visuals

Participants were most excited by the experience of an online voice command navigation.

2. Key Information

Participants felt that Price, Simple Language and more visual content as opposed to videos were important in this type of application.

3. Preparation

Participants were more than aware of existing Speech recognition platforms not being able to recognise local accents and idioms. Participants felt that some existing tutorials were not created for beginners, the language was too advanced, became mis-understood and therefore increased the learning curve and was a leading reason for abandonment.

Ideate

Brain Storming

Using the research data, I devised a brainstorming session outlining useful key features the site would need to be beneficial. Due to time constraints some where deemed to be out of scope for this project.

  1. Clear and engaging design: Use visuals that showcase the essence of playing the guitar, such as high-quality images or videos of skilled guitarists. Employ a visually appealing color scheme that catches the user's attention and reflects the energy of playing music.
  2. User-friendly navigation: Make sure that the navigation menu is intuitive and easy to use. Categorize the tutorials based on skill level or genre, allowing users to find the content they're interested in quickly and efficiently.
  3. Concise and informative headlines: Use descriptive and enticing headlines for each tutorial, clearly stating what users can expect to learn. This helps users identify the content that matches their interests and skill level.
  4. Interactive and engaging elements: Incorporate interactive elements into the landing page, such as playable audio samples or interactive chord diagrams. This allows users to have a hands-on experience and get a feel for the lessons before hand.
  5. Progress tracking: Implement a feature that allows users to track their progress through the tutorials. This could include a checklist or a progress bar that motivates users to continue learning and provides a sense of achievement. Out Of Scope
  6. Community and social features: Foster a sense of community by including features like a forum or a comment section where users can connect, ask questions, and share their progress. This encourages user engagement and can lead to a more enjoyable and collaborative learning experience. Out Of Scope
  7. Clear subscription or payment options: If the tutorials require a subscription or payment, clearly outline the different plans or pricing options available. Provide a simple and secure payment process and highlight any special offers or discounts. Out Of Scope
  8. Responsive design: Ensure that the landing page is fully optimized for mobile devices, as users may access it from their mobile phones or tablets. The content and layout should adapt seamlessly to different screen sizes.
  9. Additional resources: Offer supplementary materials, such as downloadable chord charts, sheet music, or practice exercises. Providing extra resources enhances the user's learning journey and makes the landing page more valuable.
  10. User Friendly Speech Recognition Guidelines: Set commands as simple as possible, so that User's can guess them or learn quickly, this is to lower the risks of Cognitive Overload and not recognising regional accents. Engage the user by providing feedback using a confirmation dialogue with transposed text on what the was recognised. Give the User they are in control by also providing confirm or cancel if what was recognised by the algorithm was incorrect so they can try again.

Information Architecture

Information architecture (IA) is the process of organizing, structuring, and labeling information in a way that makes it easy for users to find and understand. It involves creating an effective and intuitive framework that allows users to navigate through a digital or physical product, such as a website or application.
To ensure that users can easily locate information, comprehend its meaning and significance, and ultimately achieve their goals with minimal effort and frustration. It involves carefully considering the relationship between different components of information, such as content, features, and functionality.

Technology Research

Research was conducted at this point, to discover the most suitable Javascript Library for this project. The following were found:

  • Artyom.js
  • Anyang.js
  • Voix.js
  • PocketSphinx.js

After testing the capabilities of these libraries a decision was made to use Annyang.js. The reason this library was chosen was because it was the smallest library available, had no AI technology included and was the cut above the rest in relation to responsiveness, speed and adaptation in speech recognition in comparison with the others identified.

A/B Testing

During this design iterations, the Online Tuner would need to be tested on the User to identify which of the designs would be more useful, simpler to use and have the optimal user flow in terms of conversion rate.
Option A, offers additional voice commands separate from the main navigation prompts and specific to this feature. This requires the user to interact with buttons related to a guitar string, which plays a sound bite of the selected strings note once triggered, so that the User can then compare their guitar strings note to then tune their guitar. This is can be achieved by both manual or vocal interaction.
These extra steps and voice commands could potentially increase User confusion, learning curve and some frustration.
Option B, offers an alternative dynamic. This Tuner utilises the browser accessing the devices microphone, this then analyses the note played by the User and accurately calculates the note. The UI displays this to the user using a chromatic metric. Once the tuner identifies the note is correctly tuned, an identifier signifying this is displayed.
This is provided by a 3rd party library, could potentially conflict with the selected library for the Speech Recognition library and cause catastrophe. From a positive perspective, would be much simpler for the User.

Interactive Prototype

To demonstrate the proposed flow, I generated this interactive prototype. It demonstrates how the browser reacts on user interacts vocally with the website. During testing, the user could only navigate vocally using the prompts from the Voice Command hub on the website designs. The test facilitator transferred their commands manually to demonstrate to the user how the browser would react from the users commands.

Test Task Flow

  • Open the Command Hub using the prompt.
  • Locate the command and dictate to the system that you would like to go to the Basics Lesson 1 page.
  • Open the Command Hub again using the method in step 1.
  • Locate the command and dictate that you would like to scroll to the bottom of the page.
  • Using your own intuition from the last command, dictate what you would naturally say, to tell the system, that you would like to scroll to the top of the page.
  • Again, using your intuition, dictate what you would naturally say, to tell the system that you would want to go back to the home page.

Usability Testing

To evaluate the efficiency of this part of the application process, usability tests were conducted on 5 participants using Screen recording together with the Think Aloud protocol to collect the Qualitative data. User feedback was collected via a voluntary survey after the testing was complete to collect the Quantitative data. This data was then Triangulated to answer the overarching aim to measure the feasibility of navigating a website using a non AI Voice Controlled Website. This is the Design phase to warrant the same test on a developed website. These tests were facilitated in confidence and wil never be shared. All users managed to complete the task and could manage to recall the prompts to scroll to the top of the page and return to the home page. One area that 2 out of the 5 participants found of a slight annoyance, was the confirmation modal appearing on each interaction. It was not recorded with the majority of users and therefore did not warrant a removal, although it was minded.

After Thoughts

This UX case study helped me gain a thorough understanding of design, research, testing, and constant iteration processes. I chose to complete this project as a tutorial as I teach guitar and had a solid foundation to begin the project, having had feedback from my students over the years. I wanted to provide all students a free study aid to help them in this area, one that would provide the most use of basic tutorial material that would not cause the Users to remove their guitar from them to turn the page of a book or a website. This would hopefully lower the learning curve by eliminating some of the distractions, of what can be frustrating for most beginners learning to play the guitar.

I am glad to learn that even by making small changes, made it possible to yield beneficial impacts and create a great experience for users. It is so important to always validate our assumptions through testing. Designing this way ensures a speedy development and launching of a website or application.
