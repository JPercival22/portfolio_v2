Project Introduction

Background

Just Speak Guitar Lessons is a vocally navigable website using a non AI speech recognition algorithm. The inspiration came from a professional UX Designer meeting (courtesy of UX Circus Ltd) concerning advancements in Speech recognition and how including this improves Accessability and increases User engagement.

Problem

There are already a number of Guitar Tutorials in the public domain, each with their own niche. The challenge lies in creating a unique feature that the Users want to engage and react with that would improve their engagement and decrease the learning curve.

Solution

By having a deep understanding of user behaviour and applying a design sprint methodology with regular feedback stages, ideas were rapidly developed and validated. Empathise with those who are learning and those who have experience with products already on the market, understand their frustrations and desires.

Research 1.1: Competitive Analysis and Market Research

Understanding the Industry Landscape

I began the design process by conducting research to better understand the online Guitar Tutorials structure and standards. Key features were also compared to see what problems they presented. Personal discoveries were found, that the majority of the tutorials provided sub-standard basic lessons unless one was willing to pay for premium access, the language was aimed more towards advanced students. Further into the research I wanted to see how the public would react to introducing voice command navigation to help ease cognitive overload and reduce the learning curve, by minimising unnecessary movements.

Early Hypothesis

An early assumption here, was that the access premium, could be the reason that many beginners, decide not to continue with their learning.

Synthesised data from User Interviews confirmed this assumption.

Key Finding from Competitive Analysis

No website offers Voice command navigation so in the next phase User Interviews were conducted to see if this would be Useful, Usable and Desired.

Research 1.2: User Interviews

Understanding User Expectations

Five participants were interviewed (4 Male and 1 Female) over Two phases. The majority were aged between the ages of 18 and 22 years old. Three participants had played guitar for a while and had a lot of experience using online tutorials and two participants had never played a guitar but had always wanted to learn how to.

Research revealed that participants often prefer to interact with internet products, the general consensus was via a mobile device. All participants agreed that having voice navigable feature would certainly make learning easier. Participants who had previous experience with online tutorials, shared their learning journey behaviors, their thoughts and feelings surrounding learning to play the guitar and their experiences with using guitar tutorials, highlighting pain points and elements they thought should have been there for a beginner. All participants confirmed my initial assumption by agreeing the Premium to access the basic of lessons put them off and in most cases caused abandonment.

Insights from User Research: Key Findings and Analysis

Visuals: Participants were most excited by the experience of an online voice command navigation, emphasizing the appeal of a seamless and hands-free interaction with the application. The use of compelling visual elements enhanced the overall user experience, contributing to heightened engagement and ease of use.

Key Information: Participants emphasized the importance of multiple factors in such applications, including the competitive pricing, the use of clear and simple language, and the prominence of visual content over videos. They underscored the significance of these elements in fostering a user-friendly environment and ensuring that the application caters to a diverse audience while offering a smooth and straightforward experience.

Preparation: Participants were acutely aware of the limitations of existing speech recognition platforms in understanding local accents and idioms, stressing the need for enhanced inclusivity and adaptability to regional language nuances. Furthermore, they highlighted the challenges posed by advanced tutorials, noting that beginner-unfriendly language often led to misunderstandings and increased the learning curve, ultimately resulting in user abandonment. Additionally, participants expressed a clear preference for beginner-centric tutorials that offer a gradual learning curve, aligning with the needs of users at different skill levels and fostering sustained engagement with the application.

Research 1.3: User Persona Development

Leveraging Research Insights for Effective User Understanding

To distill the insights obtained from the User Interview Research, we meticulously crafted a user persona that encapsulates the characteristics of our target user group. This comprehensive persona provides a profound understanding of their needs, aspirations, and challenges, enabling us to foster empathy and gain valuable insights into the core concerns of our users. Leveraging this strategic tool empowers us to make design decisions that are precision-tailored to meet the exact requirements of our users, ensuring that our solutions resonate effectively with their needs.

Strategy Ideation for Elevating the User Experience

Brainstorming: Enhancing User Interaction and Engagement

Leveraging the insights from our research, I led a brainstorming session to identify key features crucial for enhancing the site's user experience. As we had time constraints, some features were deemed out of scope for this project.

  1. Clear and Engaging Design: Incorporate visuals that encapsulate the essence of guitar playing, such as high-quality images or videos of skilled guitarists. Implement a visually appealing color scheme to captivate users and reflect the vibrancy of music.
  2. User-Friendly Navigation: Develop an intuitive navigation menu for efficient content discovery, categorizing tutorials based on skill level or genre.
  3. Concise and Informative Headlines: Craft descriptive and compelling headlines for each tutorial to attract users and clearly communicate the content's relevance.
  4. Interactive and Engaging Elements: Integrate interactive features into the landing page, such as playable audio samples or interactive chord diagrams, offering users a hands-on experience.
  5. Progress Tracking: Implement a feature enabling users to track their tutorial progress, fostering motivation and a sense of accomplishment. (Out of Scope for this Project)
  6. Community and Social Features: Foster a sense of community through features like forums or comment sections to encourage user engagement and collaboration. (Out of Scope for this Project)
  7. Clear Subscription or Payment Options: Clearly outline subscription or payment plans and ensure a simple and secure payment process. Highlight any special offers or discounts. (Out of Scope for this Project)
  8. Responsive Design: Ensure seamless optimization for mobile devices, catering to users accessing the site from various screens.
  9. Additional Resources: Provide supplementary materials, such as downloadable chord charts or practice exercises, enriching the user's learning journey and adding value to the landing page.
  10. User-Friendly Speech Recognition Guidelines: Simplify commands to minimize cognitive overload, enhancing user engagement. Provide clear feedback and give users control with confirmation and correction options for recognized speech. This ensures a seamless and empowering user experience.

Interaction Design 1.2

Speech Recognition Technology Analysis

Extensive research was conducted to identify the most suitable front-end development JavaScript library for this application. As a passionate developer with extensive knowledge in semantic HTML and CSS but limited experience in JavaScript, I sought a library with a user-friendly learning curve.

After thorough research, the following JavaScript libraries were considered:

  • Artyom
  • Anyang
  • Howler
  • Google Speech

Research Findings

Following comprehensive analysis and evaluation of each option, it became evident that despite being an established framework by Apple for IOS app development, Swift UI proved unsuitable due to its incompatibility with the preferred developer language within the company. Consequently, React Native, React, and MUI emerged as more favorable choices for several compelling reasons:

After testing the capabilities of these libraries, Annyang.js was selected due to its compact size, lack of AI technology, and its superior responsiveness, speed, and accuracy in speech recognition compared to the other options.

  1. The library's compact size offers a distinct advantage over other options, promising an enhanced user experience by reducing both page load times and network strain.

  2. Annyang leverages advanced Artificial Intelligence (AI) technology, aligning seamlessly with the project's overarching objectives.

  3. While lacking in AI capabilities, research underscores Annyang's exceptional responsiveness, attributable to its sophisticated yet streamlined algorithm.

  4. A wealth of skilled developers is readily available in the market, often at more competitive rates.

  5. Annyang boasts significantly improved recognition speed compared to its competitors, positioning it as a standout choice.

  6. The thriving online community surrounding Annyang is a testament to its commitment to continuous improvement, indicative of frequent updates and enhancements.

  7. Impressively, Annyang offers robust cross-platform compatibility, ensuring seamless functionality across both Android and iOS devices.

Low-Fidelity Designs 1.3(b)

A/B Testing

During the design iterations, the Online Tuner underwent A/B testing to determine which design would be more user-friendly, improve user flow, and enhance conversion rates.

You can click any image to enlarge them.

Option A

Option A provides additional voice commands and specific prompts related to guitar string tuning. Users can interact with buttons to trigger sound bites of selected strings' notes, enabling manual or vocal interaction for tuning. However, these additional steps and voice commands might lead to user confusion, a steeper learning curve, and potential frustration.

Test A

Caption: Mp4 Guitar Tuner prototype.

Option B

Option B offers a dynamic alternative by utilizing the device's microphone to analyze the user's played note and display it using a chromatic metric. Once the tuner identifies the correct note, an indicator is displayed. While this approach relies on a 3rd party library and could potentially conflict with the selected Speech Recognition library, it offers a simpler experience for the user.

Test B

Caption: Microphone assisted prototype.

Outcomes

Following comprehensive testing with 5 participants, it was determined that both designs demonstrated significant utility, presenting a remarkable challenge in the decision-making process. The tied results reinforced the effectiveness and efficiency of both options, leading to the inclusion of both designs in the final product.

The parity in participant responses underscored the strengths of each design, highlighting their individual merits and value propositions. This outcome necessitated a balanced integration of both designs, leveraging their unique advantages to deliver a comprehensive and impactful user experience.

Hi-Fidelity Designs 1.4

Interactive Prototype

This interactive prototype, designed in Adobe XD, was instrumental in testing the task flow for our project. It effectively showcased how the browser responds to vocal user interactions with the website. During testing, users were able to navigate the website vocally using prompts from the Voice Command hub within the website designs. Additionally, our test facilitator manually transferred their commands to demonstrate how the browser would respond to the user's vocal commands.

Click to view prototype in a new window.

Task

  • Open the Command Hub using the prompt.
  • Locate the command and dictate to the system that you would like to go to the Basics Lesson 1 page.
  • Open the Command Hub again using the method in step 1.
  • Locate the command and dictate that you would like to scroll to the bottom of the page.
  • Using your own intuition from the last command, dictate what you would naturally say, to tell the system, that you would like to scroll to the top of the page.
  • Again, using your intuition, dictate what you would naturally say, to tell the system that you would want to go back to the home page.

Additionally, a separate survey was conducted to record what users would naturally say to navigate through the site. This provided a general consensus to include for the voice command system.

Usability Testing

In order to evaluate the efficiency of this aspect of the application process, usability tests were conducted on 5 participants. Screen recording was utilized in conjunction with the Think Aloud protocol to collect qualitative data. Additionally, user feedback was gathered through a voluntary survey after the testing was complete to collect quantitative data. This data was then triangulated to address the overarching aim of measuring the feasibility of navigating a website using a non-AI Voice Controlled Website. This design phase is crucial to warrant the same test on a developed website. The tests were conducted in a confidential manner and will never be shared. All users successfully completed the task and were able to recall the prompts to scroll to the top of the page and return to the home page. An area of slight annoyance identified by 2 out of the 5 participants was the appearance of a confirmation modal on each interaction. Although this was not recorded with the majority of users and therefore did not warrant removal, it was duly noted.

After Thoughts

This UX case study has provided me with a comprehensive understanding of design, research, testing, and constant iteration processes. I chose to undertake this project as a tutorial, drawing from my experience as a guitar teacher and the valuable feedback received from my students over the years. My primary objective was to offer all students a free study aid that would provide the most useful basic tutorial material without requiring them to put down their guitar to turn the page of a book or a website. This approach aimed to lower the learning curve by minimizing potential distractions, which tend to frustrate beginners learning to play the guitar.

I am pleased to have discovered that even small changes can yield significant benefits and create a great experience for users. It is imperative to consistently validate our assumptions through testing. Designing in this manner ensures the speedy development and launch of a website or application.

Next Steps

  • Continuously develop and test the free web service.
  • Plan periodic user interviews to continually improve the site.
  • Optimize the codebase for an optimal user experience.
  • Consider a framework if the site requires expansion or application version.
