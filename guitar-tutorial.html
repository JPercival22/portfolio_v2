Project Introduction

Background

Just Speak Guitar Lessons is a user-friendly website that utilizes a non-AI speech recognition algorithm for navigation. The inspiration for this innovation came from a professional UX Designer meeting, hosted by UX Circus Ltd, which focused on advancements in speech recognition technology. Incorporating this feature not only enhances accessibility but also increases user engagement. This project was also my final piece for earning a First Class BSc degree in Web Design and Development, showcasing my expertise and commitment to creating inclusive digital experiences.

Problem

While there are numerous guitar tutorials available online, each catering to different niches, the challenge is to create a unique feature that stands out. This feature should captivate users, enhance their engagement, and simplify the learning process. Additionally, it should enable hands-free use and improve accessibility for learners with accessibility issues.

Solution

Additionally, the project underwent rigorous peer review, ensuring its quality and effectiveness.

Research

Introduction

When designing a voice command tutorial platform tailored for learner guitarists, our primary focus was directed towards our established client base as the target demographic. Our approach centered on conducting comprehensive user research to gain profound insights into their distinct needs, preferences, and online behaviors. By thoroughly understanding their motivations, pain points, and expectations related to existing online tutorials, we were well-equipped to craft a user experience characterized by seamless navigation and an optimized customer journey.

Initial Assumptions

Based on my extensive background using these tutorials myself while learning the guitar, an initial hypothesis emerged, suggesting that users would derive significant value from a streamlined navigation system, prominent placement of critical pages and controls within an easily accessible prompt system, high-resolution media and detailed rich content.

Research 1.1: Competitive Analysis and Market Research

Understanding the Industry Landscape

Initiating the design process, I delved into comprehensive research to grasp the structure and standards of online guitar tutorials. Through meticulous comparison of key features, I aimed to identify prevailing issues. This phase revealed that many tutorials offered basic lessons of sub-standard quality unless users opted for premium access. Additionally, the language used often catered to advanced students, potentially causing cognitive overload and hindering the learning curve. In response, I explored the potential of introducing voice command navigation to alleviate these challenges.

Early Hypothesis

An early assumption was that the requirement for premium access might discourage many beginners from continuing their learning journey. Synthesizing data from user interviews confirmed this hypothesis.

A Competitive Research Table highlighting the Market and Competitive Analysis of existing Guitar Tutorial Websites was created, showcasing discovered insights.

Key Findings from Competitive Analysis

An intriguing discovery was made during the competitive analysis: no existing website offered voice command navigation. Subsequently, I conducted user interviews to assess the potential usefulness, usability, and desirability of this feature in the next phase of research.

Research 1.2: User Interviews & Surveys

Understanding User Expectations

Conducted in two phases, interviews were carried out with five random participants (4 male and 1 female), primarily aged between 18 and 22 years old. The decision to involve five participants aligns with established usability research practices, such as those advocated by Nielsen Norman Group, which suggest that five participants are often sufficient to uncover the majority of usability issues. Among the participants, three were experienced guitar players who frequently used online tutorials, while two had no prior guitar experience but expressed a desire to learn.

The research unveiled that participants primarily favored interacting with internet products via mobile devices. Consensus was reached among all participants on the significant enhancement a voice-navigable feature would bring to the learning experience. Participants with prior experience shared insights into their learning journey, highlighting pain points and expressing dissatisfaction with the requirement for premium access to basic lessons, often resulting in abandonment.

Insights from User Research: Key Findings and Analysis

Visuals

Participants expressed particular enthusiasm for the experience of online voice command navigation, highlighting the appeal of seamless, hands-free interaction with the application. Interaction Design 1.2

Speech Recognition Technology Analysis

Extensive research was conducted to identify the most suitable front-end development JavaScript library for this application. As a passionate developer with extensive knowledge in semantic HTML and CSS but limited experience in JavaScript, I sought a library with a user-friendly learning curve.

Identified Libraries:

  1. Artyom
  2. Annyang
  3. Howler
  4. Google Speech

Research Findings

Following a comprehensive analysis and evaluation of each option, it became clear that, while Artyom, Howler, and Google's Speech API are globally successful JavaScript libraries, they all presented a steep learning curve. Given the tight deadline of this project, where I was solely responsible for all design, development, and testing as part of my Research and Development Dissertation guidelines set by the University, a more accessible solution was necessary.

After rigorously testing the capabilities of these libraries, Annyang.js was selected for its compact size, absence of AI complexity, and superior responsiveness, speed, and accuracy in speech recognition compared to the other options.

Compact Size:

Annyang.js’s compact size offers a significant advantage, enhancing user experience by reducing page load times and network strain.

AI-Free Simplicity:

Although it lacks AI capabilities, Annyang’s exceptional responsiveness is due to its sophisticated yet streamlined algorithm.

Developer Availability:

There is a substantial pool of skilled developers familiar with Annyang, often available at competitive rates.

Gentle Learning Curve:

There is an abundance of React developers available at a lower cost in the market.

Recognition Speed:

Annyang exhibits significantly faster recognition speeds compared to its competitors, making it a standout choice.

Community Support:

The robust online community around Annyang ensures continuous improvement and frequent updates.

Cross-Platform Compatibility:

Annyang offers seamless functionality across both Android and iOS devices, ensuring broad usability.

Low-Fidelity Designs 1.3(b)

A/B Testing

During the design iterations, the Online Tuner underwent A/B testing to determine which design would be more user-friendly, improve user flow, and enhance conversion rates.

You can click any image to enlarge them.

Option A

Option A provides additional voice commands and specific prompts related to guitar string tuning. Users can interact with buttons to trigger sound bites of selected strings' notes, enabling manual or vocal interaction for tuning. However, these additional steps and voice commands might lead to user confusion, a steeper learning curve, and potential frustration.

Test A

Mp4 Guitar Tuner prototype.

Option B

Option B offers a dynamic alternative by utilizing the device's microphone to analyze the user's played note and display it using a chromatic metric. Once the tuner identifies the correct note, an indicator is displayed. While this approach relies on a 3rd party library and could potentially conflict with the selected Speech Recognition library, it offers a simpler experience for the user.

Test B

Microphone assisted prototype.

Outcomes

Following comprehensive testing with 5 participants, it was determined that both designs demonstrated significant usability, both presented a remarkable challenge in the decision-making process. The tied results reinforced the effectiveness and efficiency of both options, leading to the inclusion of both designs in the final product to enhance usability of the site and lower the learning curve of this element of learning to tune a guitar.

The parity in participant responses underscored the strengths of each design, highlighting their individual merits and value propositions. This outcome necessitated a balanced integration of both designs, leveraging their unique advantages to deliver a comprehensive and impactful user experience.

Think Aloud Protocol

The Think Aloud Protocol is a widely used method in usability testing that involves participants verbalising their thoughts, feelings, and actions while interacting with a product. This technique provides valuable insights into users' cognitive processes, allowing designers and researchers to understand how users approach tasks, identify potential usability issues, and gain a deeper understanding of the user experience. For our Voice-Controlled Guitar Tutorial high-fidelity design prototype, employing the Think Aloud Protocol helps to capture real-time feedback and pinpoint areas for improvement.

As this testing was ethical all participants were promised that their personal data would never be disclosed, this is honored and as such, all recording and screen shots is not displayed here.

What It Involves

In a Think Aloud Protocol session, participants are asked to use the Voice-Controlled Guitar Tutorial prototype and articulate their thoughts and actions as they navigate through the interface. Here’s how it typically works:

Briefing: Participants are briefed about the purpose of the session and instructed to vocalise everything that comes to their mind as they interact with the prototype.

Task Execution: Participants perform specific tasks, such as starting a guitar lesson, using voice commands to navigate and access features.

Observation and Recording:Facilitators observe and record the session, noting participants' verbal comments, actions, and any difficulties they encounter.

Follow-Up Questions: After the session, participants may be asked follow-up questions to clarify their thoughts or provide additional feedback on specific aspects of the prototype.

Pros and Cons

Pros

  1. Rich Qualitative Data: The Think Aloud Protocol provides detailed qualitative data on users' thought processes, revealing insights that are often missed in traditional usability testing.
  2. Immediate Feedback: Real-time verbalisation of thoughts allows designers to quickly identify and address usability issues, improving the design iteratively.
  3. Understanding User Perspective: By hearing users' thoughts, designers can better understand the user's perspective, leading to more user-centric design solutions.
  4. Uncovering Hidden Issues: Participants may articulate frustrations or confusion that aren't obvious through observation alone, highlighting hidden usability problems.

Cons

  1. Influence on Behavior: Verbalising thoughts can alter how participants interact with the prototype, potentially leading to unnatural behaviour.
  2. Participant Discomfort: Some participants may find it challenging or uncomfortable to think aloud, which can affect the quality of the data collected.
  3. Data Interpretation: Analysing think-aloud data can be time-consuming and requires careful interpretation to extract meaningful insights.
  4. Limited Quantitative Data: The method primarily provides qualitative data, which may need to be supplemented with other techniques to obtain comprehensive usability metrics.

After Thoughts

This UX case study has given me a deep understanding of design processes, research methodologies, and the importance of rigorous testing and iteration. Drawing from my experience as a guitar teacher and the feedback from my students, I embarked on this project with a clear goal.

My primary aim was to create a user-friendly study aid for aspiring guitarists, offering essential tutorial materials without disrupting their practice sessions. By minimizing distractions and simplifying the learning process, I sought to lower the entry barrier for beginners and enhance their overall experience.
