Background

Just Speak Guitar Lessons is a vocally navigable website using a non AI speech recognition algorithm. The inspiration came from a UX meet-up concerning advancements in Speech recognition and how including this improves Accessability and increases User engagement.

Problem

There are already a number of Guitar Tutorials in the public domain, each with their own niche. The challenge lies in creating a unique feature that the Users want to engage and react with that would improve their engagement and decrease the learning curve.

My Role

UX Researcher, UX & UI Designer Branding, Front End Development and User Testing Facilitator

Solution

By having a deep understanding of user behaviour and applying a design sprint methodology with regular feedback stages, ideas were rapidly developed and validated. Empathise with those who are learning and those who have experience with products already on the market, understand their frustrations and desires.

Market Research & Competitive Analysis

I began the design process by conducting research to better understand the online Guitar Tutorials structure and standards. Key features were also compared to see what problems they presented. Personal discoveries were found, that the majority of the tutorials provided sub-standard basic lessons unless one was willing to pay for premium access, the language was aimed more towards advanced students. Further into the research I wanted to see how the public would react to introducing voice command navigation to help ease cognitive overload and reduce the learning curve, by minimising unnecessary movements.

An early assumption here, was that the access premium, could be the reason that many beginners, decide not to continue with their learning.

Synthesised data from User Interviews confirmed this assumption.

No website offers Voice command navigation so in the next phase User Interviews were conducted to see if this would be Useful, Usable and Desired.

User Interviews

5 participants were interviewed (4male and 1 female) over 2 phases. The majority were aged between 18 and 22 years old. 3 had been playing guitar for a while and had a lot of experience learning with online tutorials and 2 had never played a guitar before, but always wanted to start.

Research revealed that participants often prefer to interact with internet products, the general consensus was via a mobile device. All participants agreed that having voice navigable feature would certainly make learning easier. Participants who had previous experience with online tutorials, shared their learning journey behaviors, their thoughts and feelings surrounding learning to play the guitar and their experiences with using guitar tutorials, highlighting pain points and elements they thought should have been there for a beginner. All participants confirmed my initial assumption by agreeing the Premium to access the basic of lessons put them off and in most cases caused abandonment.

Outcomes

1. Visuals

Participants were most excited by the experience of an online voice command navigation.

2. Key Information

Participants felt that Price, Simple Language and more visual content as opposed to videos were important in this type of application.

3. Preparation

Participants were more than aware of existing Speech recognition platforms not being able to recognise local accents and idioms. Participants felt that some existing tutorials were not created for beginners, the language was too advanced, became mis-understood and therefore increased the learning curve and was a leading reason for abandonment.

Combining earlier research and data retrieved form User Research, I created a persona to create a visual representation of the target audience.

Brainstorming

Using the research data, I devised a brainstorming session outlining useful key features the site would need to be beneficial. Due to time constraints some where out of scope for the project.
  1. Clear and engaging design: Use visuals that showcase the essence of playing the guitar, such as high-quality images or videos of skilled guitarists. Employ a visually appealing color scheme that catches the user's attention and reflects the energy of playing music.
  2. User-friendly navigation: Make sure that the navigation menu is intuitive and easy to use. Categorize the tutorials based on skill level or genre, allowing users to find the content they're interested in quickly and efficiently.
  3. Concise and informative headlines: Use descriptive and enticing headlines for each tutorial, clearly stating what users can expect to learn. This helps users identify the content that matches their interests and skill level.
  4. Interactive and engaging elements: Incorporate interactive elements into the landing page, such as playable audio samples or interactive chord diagrams. This allows users to have a hands-on experience and get a feel for the lessons before hand.
  5. Progress tracking: Implement a feature that allows users to track their progress through the tutorials. This could include a checklist or a progress bar that motivates users to continue learning and provides a sense of achievement. Out Of Scope
  6. Community and social features: Foster a sense of community by including features like a forum or a comment section where users can connect, ask questions, and share their progress. This encourages user engagement and can lead to a more enjoyable and collaborative learning experience. Out Of Scope
  7. Clear subscription or payment options: If the tutorials require a subscription or payment, clearly outline the different plans or pricing options available. Provide a simple and secure payment process and highlight any special offers or discounts. Out Of Scope
  8. Responsive design: Ensure that the landing page is fully optimized for mobile devices, as users may access it from their mobile phones or tablets. The content and layout should adapt seamlessly to different screen sizes.
  9. Additional resources: Offer supplementary materials, such as downloadable chord charts, sheet music, or practice exercises. Providing extra resources enhances the user's learning journey and makes the landing page more valuable.
