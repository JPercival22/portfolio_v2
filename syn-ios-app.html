Project Introduction

Background

Synalogik Innovative Solutions Ltd is a UK-based data aggregator supporting government, police, and national security through an online platform that identifies persons of interest. It also serves the public sector, including banks, betting, and insurance organisations.

At a pivotal growth stage, I was appointed as the sole UX Designer. My role involved devising a marketing strategy that enabled existing customers to discover and order additional relevant data sets, enhancing workflows and driving sales.

I led strategic consultations and the design of Synalogik’s e-commerce platform. Recognising the need for clear design specifications and front-end standards, I developed UX strategies and integrated marketing efforts to align with both user and business objectives. This case study focuses on the marketing strategy project.
Content has been adjusted to maintain confidentiality and protect the company’s design.

Problem

The organisation had set its own initial goals and requirements, including ideation on integrating a live Twitter feed to boost brand awareness. However, there was a lack of research into client needs and preferences. The business was focused on its own objectives and lacked prior design experience, making my role particularly challenging.

The business required an e-commerce platform where existing customers could securely log in with their credentials. The platform would then systematically provide relevant data sets from various providers based on the user’s use case. Customers would have the option to select different tiers, each offering advanced data sets at reasonable price points.

Due to COVID-19 travel restrictions, all client and stakeholder meetings had to be held online. As a startup with financial constraints, conducting user interviews and testing during the design phase required careful planning to manage costs effectively. This added complexity to gathering user requirements and incorporating them into the design.

Solution

Using design sprint methodology and insights into user behaviour, we designed and validated ideas, incorporating ongoing feedback from clients and stakeholders. We prioritised empathy and integrated marketing strategies to create a seamless experience throughout the user journey.

E-commerce strategies included personalised product recommendations, tiered pricing with clear value, and targeted email marketing. We also streamlined the checkout process to increase conversions.

To optimise costs, we used popular industry tools including, Microsoft Teams, Google Slides, and Figma for design and testing. We adhered to Web Accessibility Standards and provided detailed specifications for the Development Team, ensuring consistency across Front-End Frameworks.

Research

When designing an e-commerce platform for professional analysts, we focused on our existing client base. Our approach centred on in-depth user research to understand their needs, preferences, and online behaviours. By identifying their motivations, pain points, and expectations, we crafted a user experience with seamless navigation and an optimised shopping journey.

Initial Assumptions

Drawing from my experience, the initial hypothesis was that users would benefit from a streamlined navigation system, easy access to key pages in the footer, detailed product descriptions, and an efficient, user-friendly checkout process.

Research 1.1

Understanding User Expectations

We conducted user interviews to gain insights into expectations for online shopping. Engaging a diverse range of users, we explored:

  • Desires: What users want from an online shopping experience.
  • Pain Points: Challenges and frustrations users encounter while shopping online.
  • Preferences: Specific features users prefer when navigating retail sites.
  • Device: Our research revealed that most clients provided high-level employees with Apple products, so the design adhered to iOS standards for the prototype. Future development would expand to support all devices.

This approach helped us validate our initial assumptions by understanding users' behaviours, thoughts, emotions, and frustrations. The insights gathered provided a clear view of the challenges and opportunities for creating an effective platform.

These findings were key in shaping the design and development of our platform, ensuring it met user expectations and provided a seamless experience.

Front End Technology Analysis

Selecting the right front-end framework was crucial for several reasons. Firstly, it was essential to minimise costs and development time, ensuring efficient use of resources, particularly since the organisation had limited experience in front-end development. This choice directly impacted both the project's budget and timeline.

  • Alignment with Team Expertise: The framework needed to integrate well with the team’s existing skills in JAVA and JavaScript, reducing the learning curve and enabling faster development.
  • Learning Curve: As the team was not deeply experienced in front-end technologies, a user-friendly framework was essential to ensure rapid adoption and minimise delays.
  • Future Scalability: To support the development of iOS (and potentially Android) applications, the selected framework had to facilitate a unified codebase, making it easier to scale and adapt to future needs without incurring additional overhead.

Ultimately, this approach allowed for more efficient resource allocation, reducing unnecessary costs while laying a strong foundation for future development.

Identified Frameworks:

  • Swift UI (Apple Documentation)
  • UIKit (Apple Documentation)
  • React Native (Documentation)
  • React (Documentation)
  • MUI (Documentation)

Research Findings

After a thorough evaluation of each potential framework, it became evident that Swift UI, despite being an established choice for iOS development, was not suitable for our needs due to its incompatibility with the team’s preferred developer language. Consequently, React Native , React , and MUI emerged as the most advantageous options for the following reasons:

  • Availability and Affordability of Developers: React has a significantly higher availability of developers compared to Swift, with a promising 5:1 ratio, making it a strong investment for future ROI.

  • Utilisation of JavaScript, HTML5, and CSS3: React relies on widely-used languages like JavaScript, HTML5, and CSS3, providing a familiar and gentle learning curve for developers.

  • Gentle Learning Curve: React’s widespread use and lower market cost for developers make it a highly accessible framework for the team.

  • Modern Web Standards: React components adhere to modern web standards, enhancing customisability and promoting efficient development through reusable components and reduced code.

  • Extensive Library of Components and Icons: React offers an extensive library of pre-built components and icon libraries, speeding up development time and improving consistency.

  • Cross-Platform Compatibility: React Native supports both iOS and Android development, enabling a unified codebase for both platforms and streamlining future app development.

During the refinement stages, user involvement increased significantly as we aligned Landing/Index Page Marketing Strategies—such as the Unique Selling Proposition (USP), Strip Banner, and Primary Banner—with stakeholder consensus. UX Testing 1.2

Testing Procedure

Usability testing evaluates how effectively users interact with a design. For this prototype, we employed the System Usability Scale (SUS) as the foundation for testing, combining Task-Based Usability Testing and First Click Testing . Following the SUS evaluation, we conducted a user survey to capture additional qualitative feedback. This comprehensive approach ensured we gathered actionable insights into user interactions and the overall experience.

Thirty participants, representing a diverse range of user backgrounds, completed time based (6 minutes) tasks using the interactive Figma prototype. These tasks focused on key areas, such as navigation, task efficiency, and decision-making. The SUS scoring provided quantifiable insights into usability, complemented by a survey to gather subjective user perspectives.

Advantages of Task-Based Usability Testing

  • Focused Insights: Helps pinpoint specific usability challenges within key tasks.
  • Measurable Results: Captures quantifiable metrics, such as task completion time and error rates.
  • Direct Feedback: Observing users in real-time highlights obstacles and usability gaps.

Challenges

  • Limited Scope: Focuses on individual tasks, potentially missing broader usability issues.
  • Contextual Gaps: Task-specific testing may overlook the holistic user experience.

Task-Based Usability Testing is invaluable for fine-tuning high-fidelity designs, offering actionable insights into how users interact with the product. By carefully observing and analysing how users complete tasks, designers can make informed improvements to enhance usability and the overall user experience.

Results and Analysis

The testing phase involved 30 participants, all engaging in the task-based evaluation using the System Usability Score (SUS) procedure, whilst incorporating the First Click and Time Based Tasks. Each participant completed the tasks while verbalising their thoughts, which provided valuable insights into their navigation process, ease of interaction, and overall experience with the prototype, using the System Usability Scale (SUS).

The System Usability Scale (SUS) assessment yielded an impressive score of 88 out of 100, significantly exceeding the benchmark of 68. This outstanding score underscores the user-centric effectiveness of the design, indicating that it resonates well with the target audience and affirming its overall usability.

Task 1 - Navigation Ease and Efficiency

This test evaluated the usability of the application, from the Home screen to adding an item to the basket and completing the checkout. The task was time-based (6 minutes), with a pre-selected product to simplify the process. The objective was to determine whether navigation was intuitive and straightforward.

  • Results:
  • All participants completed the task successfully.
  • 86% of participants finished within the time limit.
  • 14% took up to 6 minutes to complete the task.
  • No participants encountered navigational issues.

Task 2 - Decision-Making Efficiency

This test assessed the usability of the product upgrade dropdown system, following the same journey as Task 1, with the additional task of upgrading the product before checkout. The dropdown components, positioned logically with dynamic pricing, were placed on the Product Description page.
From a business perspective, the goal was to increase marketing opportunities and incorporate potential decoy items post-analysis. From a user perspective, the system was designed to provide a simple, intuitive, and engaging experience to improve decision-making efficiency.

  • Results:
  • All participants completed the task successfully.
  • 68% engaged with the product upgrade system on the Product Description Page.
  • 22% engaged with the system on the Added to Basket modal.
  • 10% interacted with the system on the Basket Page.
  • The test provided sufficient opportunities for product upgrades before checkout.

Conclusion

Final Reflections

This project highlights the significance of continuous usability testing and iterative design. While the results are promising, they reveal that user needs evolve, requiring ongoing evaluation and adaptation. The positive feedback on the product upgrade system shows that users value added options, though balancing flexibility without overwhelming them is crucial.

Task-Based Usability Testing has been crucial in improving both functionality and user experience. Observing real users interact with the application provided valuable insights into design strengths and areas for improvement.

The success rates and engagement metrics indicate that users find the navigation and upgrade processes intuitive. However, feedback points to areas for further optimisation, such as ensuring timely task completion and enhancing user interaction for better engagement.

This project reinforces the need for ongoing usability testing and design iteration. While promising, the results highlight the necessity of evolving designs to meet changing user needs. The product upgrade system's positive reception illustrates the value of additional options, but maintaining balance is key.

Overall, this UX project has provided a comprehensive understanding of how the high-fidelity design meets user needs. Moving forward, we will prioritise user-centric design and continuous testing to ensure the application remains intuitive, efficient, and enjoyable for all users.
