Introduction

Background

I was responsible for leading strategic consultations and enhancing the platform, including designing the e-commerce platform at Synalogik. Despite receiving the Prince's Award for Innovation in 2022, I recognized the need for defined design specifications and front-end standards as the company navigated its early stages. I developed UX strategies for the e-commerce app and integrated marketing strategies to align with user and business needs.

Problem

The organization had identified their goals and set forth requirements to align with them, one suggestion was to integrate a live Twitter feed within the application to promote Brand Awareness. However, the research conducted regarding the specific needs and desires of their clients was limited. It is worth mentioning that all client and stakeholder meetings had to be conducted online due to travel limitations and COVID-19. As a startup with financial constraints, conducting user interviews and testing during the design stages required careful consideration and strategic planning to manage expenses effectively.

    • Solution

    Utilizing a deep understanding of user behavior and employing design sprint methodology, we efficiently designed and validated ideas while incorporating regular feedback from clients and stakeholders. By prioritizing empathy and integrating marketing strategies throughout the user journey, we strategically created a seamless experience. To maximize cost-efficiency, we leveraged tools like Microsoft Teams, Google Slides, and Figma for meetings and design testing. We also ensured adherence to Web Accessibility Standards, addressing inconsistencies to deliver a uniform and inclusive user experience. Additionally, detailed specifications for the Development Team were provided through the analysis of Front End Frameworks and Standards.

    Research

    In developing an e-commerce platform tailored for professional analysts, our primary focus was set on our established client base as the target demographic. Our approach revolved around in-depth user research, enabling us to gain profound insights into their distinct needs, preferences, and online behaviors. By comprehensively understanding their motivations, pain points, and expectations pertaining to online shopping, we were empowered to craft a user experience characterized by seamless navigation and an optimized shopping journey. Informed by these insights, we pursued the development of a platform meticulously attuned to the specific demands of our professional analyst clientele and their business goals.

    Hypothesis

    Given my extensive background, an initial hypothesis emerged, suggesting that users would derive significant value from a streamlined navigation system, prominent placement of critical pages within an easily accessible footer, detailed product descriptions, and notably, an efficient and user-friendly checkout process.

    User Interviews

    User interviews were conducted to gain a deep understanding of user expectations in the context of online shopping. By engaging with users from diverse backgrounds and age groups, the interviews aimed to uncover valuable insights into their desires, pain points, and preferences when it comes to their online shopping experiences. Understanding the behaviors, thoughts, emotions, and frustrations of users who have previous experience with online shopping allowed the team to validate their assumptions and gain a comprehensive view of the challenges and opportunities for designing a successful online shopping platform. These user insights are crucial for informing the design and development of the platform to ensure it meets user expectations and provides a seamless and enjoyable experience for all users.

    Insights from User Research: Key Findings and Analysis

    The user interviews yielded valuable insights regarding the design and functionality of the application, which have been distilled into key findings. These findings are structured in a simplified and logical manner, covering essential aspects of the application's user experience. The insights address critical areas such as the top bar, navigation system, home page categorization, marketing and best offers, product cards, and form design. They reflect the participants' preferences and recommendations, emphasizing the importance of a simplistic and logical navigation system, minimal text on product cards, and the need for clear and intuitive form design. These key findings serve as a dependable foundation for prioritizing improvements and refining the application to meet user needs and expectations. By integrating these valuable insights, the design team can confidently make informed decisions to enhance the user experience, resulting in a more user-friendly and engaging application.

    User Persona Development: Leveraging Research Insights for Effective User Understanding

    To articulate the insights gleaned from User Interview Research, a meticulously crafted user persona was developed. This comprehensive persona encapsulates the characteristics of our target user group, offering a deep understanding of their needs, aspirations, and challenges. By leveraging this persona, we are able to foster empathy and gain valuable insights into our users' core concerns. This strategic tool serves as a guiding force for design decisions, ensuring that our solutions are custom-tailored to meet the precise requirements of our users.

    User-Centric Customer Journey Map: Navigating the E-commerce Experience

    The customer journey map explores the various stages that a customer undergoes while navigating an e-commerce website, starting from the initial search on a search engine to reaching the user-centric pages such as product list, product description, basket, and finally checkout. This journey delves into the emotions and expectations experienced by the user as they interact with the site, providing valuable insight into their needs and behaviors. The user's emotions and expectations are crucial in understanding their perspective as they move through the different stages of the customer journey. By analyzing their emotional responses and expectations, we can identify pain points, satisfaction, and opportunities for improvement within the user experience. This helps in designing a more user-centric and engaging e-commerce platform that effectively addresses the needs and desires of the customers.

    Ideation

    To optimize the application's usability and enhance the user experience, mitigating user abandonment while fostering return visits and maximizing ROI, we have employed a diverse range of strategic methodologies. Our approach centers on integrating pertinent visual cues, iconography, and imagery within an interactive and intuitive framework. Here’s a breakdown of our implementation:

    • Visual Cues: Leveraging tooltips, progress indicators, and hover effects, we have established clear guidance and feedback mechanisms, fostering seamless navigation and comprehension for users within the system.

    • Iconography: By integrating meaningful and recognizable icons aligning with user expectations, cognitive load is minimized, and interface intuitiveness is heightened, effectively communicating intended actions and functions.

    • Imagery: Emphasizing visually compelling and contextually relevant imagery, our approach boosts user engagement and fortifies the communication of pertinent information, perpetuating an enriching user experience.

    • Interactivity: Thoughtfully incorporating interactive elements in the form of buttons, sliders, and drag-and-drop functionalities where applicable, we have elevated user engagement and incentivised exploration, thereby magnifying retention rates.

    • User-Centric Design: Our design ethos places paramount importance on user feedback and behavior, entailing meticulous testing and refinement of visual cues, iconography, and imagery to fortify interface intuitiveness and user friendliness.

    • Persuasive Product Presentation: Astutely leveraging high-quality images, detailed descriptions, and customer reviews to depict products, we have cultivated trust and conveyed value to the users, strategically highlighting key features, promotions, and discounts for an immersive product presentation.

    Notably, within the scope of the current Minimal Viable Product (MVP) design phase, considerations for personalization and social media links were deemed beyond the purview due to temporal, fiscal, and potential security implications. Additionally, incorporating a live Twitter feed was deemed unfeasible owing to identified security risks and scrupulous behaviors unearthed during the research phase.

    By embedding these indispensable UX principles, our overarching objective is to fashion an alluring, user-centric e-commerce application designed to elevate usability and stimulate user retention and engagement.

    Technology Research

    Thorough research was undertaken to determine the most appropriate Front-End Development Framework for this application. The business requirements emphasized the need for a framework that harmonizes with the development team's proficiency in JAVA and JavaScript. Considering the limited expertise in Front-End Development within our organization, it was imperative for the chosen framework to have a user-friendly learning curve.

    Furthermore, the selected framework was required to possess the capability to develop Android applications for future endeavors and enable the sharing of a unified code base, thereby facilitating a more streamlined and efficient development process.

    Identified Frameworks:

    • Swift UI (Apple Documentation).
    • UIKit (Apple Documentation)
    • React Native (Documentation)
    • React (Documentation)
    • MUI (Documentation)

    Research Findings

    After conducting thorough analysis and evaluation of each option, it became clear that despite being an established framework by Apple for IOS app development, Swift UI proved unsuitable due to its incompatibility with the preferred developer language within the company. Consequently, React Native, React, and MUI emerged as more favorable choices for several compelling reasons:

    1. The higher affordability and greater availability of React developers in the market, compared to Apple developers, offered a promising 5:1 ratio, establishing it as a sound investment for future ROI for stakeholders.
    2. React utilizes JavaScript as its coding language.
    3. It boasts a gentle learning curve.
    4. There is an abundance of developers available at a lower cost in the market.
    5. It features components that adhere to modern web standards and are easily customizable.
    6. It offers an extensive library of components and icon libraries.
    7. It is compatible for both Android and IOS application development.

    Usability Testing

    Usability testing is a critical process for evaluating the effectiveness and efficiency of a product's user interface. The System Usability Scale (SUS) is a widely used and reliable tool for measuring system usability. It comprises a 10-item questionnaire with responses rated on a 5-point Likert scale, which is then transformed into a SUS score ranging from 0 to 100.

    SUS Scores.

    To obtain these valuable insights, a group of 5 participants rigorously tested the design following the prescribed task flow under controlled test conditions. As the test facilitator, I facilitated the online testing sessions utilizing Microsoft Teams as a robust platform for scrutinizing users' paralinguistic cues while they navigated through the prototype. Following the completion of the tasks unaided, all participants were requested to independently complete a multiple-choice System Usability Scale (SUS) to gauge the overall usability of the design.

    Upon meticulous calculation and analysis, we are pleased to report that our design surpassed the esteemed minimal benchmark of 68/100, achieving an outstanding SUS score of 87.5/100. This resounding success unequivocally signals a user-centric design that resonates with our audience, fueling confidence in the superior quality of our product. We invite you to peruse the accompanying visuals, which include the questionnaire and the impressive final score, underscoring the unequivocal excellence of our design.

    After Thoughts

    This UX case study has provided me with a comprehensive understanding of design, research, testing, and constant iteration processes. As the sole UX Designer, UX Test Facilitator, and UI Designer at this company, I encountered challenges related to financial struggles, unrealistic time constraints, and a lack of team support. However, these obstacles served as a test of my mettle, and through determination, empathy, and careful considerations to strike the right balance between user and business needs, I successfully delivered this User-Centric and Data-Driven design.

    Given the time constraints and budgetary limitations, although the SUS Testing provided valuable insights, I would have preferred to facilitate other complementary testing methodologies to triangulate the data more comprehensively. For instance, usability testing, A/B testing, and heuristic evaluation are additional examples of methodologies that could have further enriched the insights and validation of the design decisions.
