Project Introduction

Synalogik Innovative Solutions Ltd is a UK-based data aggregator that supports UK Governments, Police, and National Security by providing an online platform for identifying persons and connected individuals of interest in the Private Sector. Additionally, they offer services to the Public Sector, including Banks, Betting, and Insurance Organisations.

The platform, named SCOUT, utilises data sets from multiple trustworthy providers, enabling rapid identification of properties, people, and companies within 60 seconds with high accuracy. In 2022, the team was nationally recognised with the prestigious Prince's Award for Innovation.

As the company was poised for growth, I was assigned as the sole UX Designer during this critical stage of its infancy. My role involved evaluating the current state of their platform, consulting on improvements, and creating a marketing strategy that allowed existing customers to view and order additional relevant data sets. This strategy aimed to enhance clients' workflow and increase Synalogik's sales.

This case study focuses on this project. Understandably, the content has been altered to protect myself from conflict of interest and to keep the company's design private.

Background

I was assigned as the sole UX Designer during this critical stage of the organisations infancy. My role involved creating a marketing strategy that allowed existing customers to view and order additional relevant data sets. This strategy aimed to enhance clients' workflow and increase Synalogik's sales.

Successfully led strategic consultations, enhancement and the design of the e-commerce platform at Synalogik. I also identified the crucial requirement for clearly defined design specifications and front-end standards during the company's initial phases. This led me to develop UX strategies for the e-commerce app and seamlessly integrate marketing strategies to harmonise with both user and business needs.

Problem

The organisation had established its goals and outlined requirements to align with them, including suggestions like incorporating a live Twitter feed within the application to bolster brand awareness. However, there was a significant lack of research into the specific needs and desires of their clients. The business was primarily focused on its own goals and had no previous design knowledge, making my role particularly challenging from the outset.

Additionally, all client and stakeholder meetings had to be conducted online due to travel restrictions and the impact of COVID pandemic outbreaks. As a startup with financial constraints, conducting user interviews and testing during the design stages required careful consideration and strategic planning to effectively manage expenses. This further complicated the process of gathering user requirements and integrating them into the design.

Solution

With a comprehensive understanding of user behaviour and the implementation of design sprint methodology, we successfully designed and validated ideas while consistently incorporating feedback from clients and stakeholders. Prioritising empathy and integrating marketing strategies throughout the user journey, we tactically developed a seamless experience.

To maximise cost-efficiency, we utilised tools such as Microsoft Teams, Google Slides, for meetings and Figma for designing and testing. We also ensured adherence to Web Accessibility Standards, addressing inconsistencies to deliver a consistent and inclusive user experience. Additionally, we furnished detailed specifications for the Development Team through the analysis of Front End Frameworks and Standards.

Research

Introduction

WhWhen designing an e-commerce platform tailored for professional analysts, we prioritised our established client base as the target demographic. Our approach centred on conducting comprehensive user research to gain deep insights into their unique needs, preferences, and online behaviours. By thoroughly understanding their motivations, pain points, and expectations related to online shopping, we were able to craft a user experience characterised by seamless navigation and an optimised shopping journey.

Initial Assumptions

Based on my extensive background, an initial hypothesis emerged that users would derive significant value from a streamlined navigation system, the prominent placement of critical pages in an easily accessible footer, detailed product descriptions, and, notably, an efficient and user-friendly checkout process.

Research 1.1: User Interviews

Understanding User Expectations

Our team conducted user interviews to explore in-depth the expectations users have in the context of online shopping. We engaged with a diverse pool of users across various backgrounds and age groups to uncover valuable insights into:

  • Desires: What users want from an online shopping experience.
  • Pain Points: The challenges and frustrations users face while shopping online.
  • Preferences: Specific features and functionalities that users prefer when navigating online retail.
  • Device: During client research, discoveries were made that the majority of clients provided high level employess with Apple products. The designs were to incorporate the IOS standards for the prototype. It was minded that the app would be eventually be developed to cater for all devices, during the development and changes to be designed later.

This tailored approach enabled us to gain a deeper understanding of user behaviours, thoughts, emotions, and frustrations from their previous online shopping experiences, thereby validating our initial assumptions. The insights gathered provided us with a comprehensive view of the challenges and opportunities involved in creating a successful online shopping platform.

These user-centric findings became pivotal in guiding the design and development of our platform, ensuring that it not only meets user expectations but also delivers a seamless and gratifying experience for all users.

Interaction Design 1.2

Front End Technology Analysis

We carried out extensive research to identify the most suitable front-end development framework for this application. The project requirements emphasised the need for a framework that aligns with the development team's expertise in JAVA and JavaScript. Given the organisation's limited experience in front-end development, it was essential to choose a framework with a user-friendly learning curve.

Additionally, it was crucial for the selected framework to support the development of IOS - eventual Android applications - for future projects and enable the sharing of a unified codebase, facilitating a more efficient and seamless development process.

Identified Frameworks:

  • Swift UI (Apple Documentation)
  • UIKit (Apple Documentation)
  • React Native (Documentation)
  • React (Documentation)
  • MUI (Documentation)

Research Findings

Following a comprehensive analysis and evaluation of each option, it became clear that, despite being an established framework by Apple for iOS app development, Swift UI proved unsuitable due to its incompatibility with the preferred developer language within the company. Consequently, React Native, React, and MUI emerged as more favourable choices for several compelling reasons:

  • Availability and Affordability of Developers: The market has a significantly higher availability of React developers compared to Apple developers, with a promising 5:1 ratio. This establishes React as a sound investment for future ROI for stakeholders.

  • Utilisation of JavaScript, HTML5, and CSS3: React utilises widely adopted languages like JavaScript, HTML5, and CSS3, providing a gentle learning curve.

  • Gentle Learning Curve: There is an abundance of React developers available at a lower cost in the market.

  • Modern Web Standards: React features components that adhere to modern web standards, are easily customisable, and make development more efficient in terms of reusability and reduction in code.

  • Extensive Library of Components and Icons: React provides a comprehensive library of components and icon libraries.

  • Cross-Platform Compatibility: React Native is compatible with both Android and iOS application development.

Index Page Design

Overview of the Index Page

The index page acts as the gateway to the e-commerce platform, meticulously designed to create an engaging and user-friendly experience from the moment users land on the site. The design prioritises both aesthetic appeal and functional efficiency, ensuring that key elements are prominently featured to enhance navigation and drive user interaction. Key components of the index page include:

  • Top Bar: The top bar is designed to provide easy access to essential features such as search functionality and user account options. It incorporates clear and concise navigation links to crucial areas of the site, ensuring users can swiftly find what they need.
  • Unique Selling Proposition (USP): Displayed prominently on the page, the USP highlights the core benefits and unique features of the e-commerce platform. This section aims to capture user attention immediately, showcasing what sets the platform apart from competitors.
  • Strip Banner: Positioned beneath the USP, the strip banner presents time-sensitive promotions or announcements. Its purpose is to draw attention to current deals or upcoming events, encouraging users to explore further.
  • Primary Banner: The primary banner, featured centrally on the page, showcases high-impact visuals and major promotional content. This element is designed to captivate users and direct them towards featured products or special offers.
  • In-Page Product Navigation: To facilitate seamless browsing, the index page includes an in-page product navigation section. This feature allows users to easily explore various product categories and collections without having to navigate away from the homepage.
  • Cross-Selling Section: Positioned strategically to encourage additional purchases, the cross-selling section highlights related products or complementary items. This feature aims to increase average order value by suggesting items that may interest the user based on their browsing behaviour.
  • Footer: The footer provides comprehensive navigation options, including links to customer service, company information, privacy policies, and social media channels. It ensures users have access to essential resources and can easily connect with the brand.

By incorporating these elements, the index page is designed to deliver a compelling and streamlined user experience. The layout is thoughtfully crafted to maximise user engagement, facilitate smooth navigation, and drive conversions through effective promotional strategies and intuitive design.

Product Page Design

Overview of the Product Page

The product page is crafted to offer a comprehensive and engaging shopping experience, combining detailed product information with intuitive navigation and interactive elements. This page is designed to help users make informed purchasing decisions while providing a smooth and enjoyable browsing experience. The key components of the product page include:

  • Top Bar: The top bar remains consistent across the site, providing essential navigation tools such as search functionality and access to user account settings. This ensures that users can effortlessly navigate to other sections of the site while browsing the product page.
  • Unique Selling Proposition (USP): Positioned at the top of the product page, the USP highlights the key benefits and differentiators of the product. This section aims to capture user interest and reinforce the value proposition of the item being viewed.
  • Strip Banner: Located beneath the USP, the strip banner draws attention to current promotions, discounts, or special offers related to the product. This feature is designed to encourage users to take advantage of deals and enhance their purchasing decision.
  • Title and Editorial: The page features a prominent title for the product, accompanied by an editorial section that provides detailed descriptions, key features, and benefits. This content is crafted to inform and engage users, helping them understand the products value and relevance.
  • Filter and Refine Options: To aid users in narrowing down their search, the product page includes filter and refine options. These tools allow users to sort and view products based on various criteria such as price range, category, and ratings, enhancing their browsing efficiency.
  • Product Cards: The product cards are designed to showcase related or recommended items, each featuring:
    • Image: A high-quality image of the product to give users a clear visual representation.
    • Title: The product name, clearly displayed for easy identification.
    • Review Count: User reviews and ratings to provide social proof and assist in decision-making.
    • Promotional Offers: Any current deals or special offers associated with the product.
    • Pricing: The products price, prominently displayed to inform users of the cost.

By integrating these elements, the product page is designed to deliver a detailed and user-centric shopping experience. The layout ensures that users have all the necessary information to make informed decisions while also providing easy access to additional products and relevant offers.

Basket Page Design

Overview of the Basket Page

The basket page serves as a crucial stage in the users shopping journey, providing a clear and transparent summary of their selected items and facilitating a smooth transition to checkout. Designed with user experience in mind, the basket page integrates essential features to enhance decision-making and streamline the purchase process. Key components of the basket page include:

  • Top Bar: Consistent with other pages, the top bar provides navigation options, including search functionality and user account access. This ensures users can easily navigate or search while reviewing their basket.
  • Unique Selling Proposition (USP): Located prominently, the USP reiterates the core benefits and unique aspects of the e-commerce platform, reinforcing the value proposition as users review their selections.
  • Strip Banner: Positioned below the USP, the strip banner highlights current promotions or discounts applicable to the basket, providing users with real-time incentives to complete their purchase.
  • Personalised "Your Basket" Title: The basket page features a personalised title, Your Basket, creating a more engaging and tailored experience for the user. This title helps users quickly identify and focus on their selections.
  • Pricing Position: Pricing information is prominently displayed to offer the earliest opportunity for transparency. This helps users understand the total cost, including any taxes or shipping fees, enabling efficient decision-making and avoiding surprises at checkout.
  • Discount Alert Section: A dedicated section for discount alerts informs users of any applicable discounts or offers. This feature encourages users to apply available savings before finalising their purchase.
  • Multi-Faceted Ordered Product Details: Each product in the basket is detailed with comprehensive information, including options for comparison marketing. This allows users to compare products and make informed choices about their selections.
  • Discount Code Section: Users can enter discount codes in this section to apply additional savings to their basket. This feature is designed to enhance user satisfaction and incentivise purchases.
  • Itemised Total Section: The itemised total section provides a breakdown of costs, including product prices, discounts, taxes, and shipping fees. This transparency ensures users are fully aware of their total expenditure before proceeding to checkout.
  • Cross-Selling Section: To further enhance the shopping experience, the cross-selling section suggests related or complementary products based on the items in the basket. This feature aims to increase basket size and offer additional value to users.
  • Footer: The footer maintains consistency with other pages, providing additional navigation options and essential links such as customer service, privacy policy, and terms of service.

The basket page is designed to provide users with a clear, comprehensive view of their selections and associated costs, while also offering opportunities for savings and additional purchases. By integrating these features, the page ensures a smooth and transparent shopping experience, encouraging users to complete their transactions with confidence.

Hi Fidelity Design 1.3

Interactive Prototype

This Figma-generated e-commerce prototype was purposefully crafted to optimize user task flow and enhance platform exploration. As part of our rigorous testing, five users were assigned three tasks, all beginning from the home page. Each task involved navigating to the product list page, selecting a product, and then choosing a different version from the product description page or confirmation modal. The test was deemed successful when users arrived at the Basket page.

User testing provided invaluable insights into several key areas, including:

Usability

Identifying any obstacles or confusing elements that users encountered, allowing us to make necessary adjustments to improve navigation and overall ease of use.

Efficiency

Usability Testing

Ensuring the usability and effectiveness of our designs through user experience testing, helped position us for the best possible UX before moving into development.

System Usability Scale (SUS)

Usability testing is a critical process for evaluating the effectiveness and efficiency of a product's user interface. The System Usability Scale (SUS) is a widely used and reliable tool for measuring system usability. It comprises a 10-item questionnaire with responses rated on a 5-point Likert scale, which is then transformed into a SUS score ranging from 0 to 100.

SUS Scores.

To obtain these valuable insights, a group of 5 participants rigorously tested the design following the prescribed task flow under controlled test conditions. As the test facilitator, I facilitated the online testing sessions utilizing Microsoft Teams as a robust platform for scrutinizing users' paralinguistic cues while they navigated through the prototype. Following the completion of the tasks unaided, all participants were requested to independently complete a multiple-choice System Usability Scale (SUS) to gauge the overall usability of the design.

Upon meticulous calculation and analysis, we are pleased to report that our design surpassed the esteemed minimal benchmark of 68/100, achieving an outstanding SUS score of 87.5/100. This resounding success unequivocally signals a user-centric design that resonates with our audience, fueling confidence in the superior quality of our product. After Thoughts

This UX case study has provided me with a comprehensive understanding of design, research, testing, and constant iteration processes. As the sole UX Designer, UX Test Facilitator, and UI Designer at this company, I encountered challenges related to financial struggles, unrealistic time constraints, and a lack of team support. However, these obstacles served as a test of my mettle, and through determination, empathy, and careful considerations to strike the right balance between user and business needs, I successfully delivered this User-Centric and Data-Driven design.

Given the time constraints and budgetary limitations, although the SUS Testing provided valuable insights, I would have preferred to facilitate other complementary testing methodologies to triangulate the data more comprehensively. For instance, usability testing, A/B testing, and heuristic evaluation are additional examples of methodologies that could have further enriched the insights and validation of the design decisions.
